Subtitle: The Fourth Tribunal

Format Note
This Chapter 5 draft continues directly from Chapter 4 end-state and is written as a full production storyboard for cinematic scripting, mission briefs, battle scripting, and character spotlight planning.
Core priority: legality and combat are the same arena.

Core Goals
- Escalate from city containment to jurisdiction war.
- Introduce a mirror governance system that contests the party's legal standing.
- Make playable onboarding for units 022-025 feel earned, risky, and systemic.
- Force Aric to prove witness governance under active legal attack, not only military attack.
- Keep every major victory two-layered: tactical survival and admissible standing.
- End with partial legal victory and a wider existential jurisdiction threat.

Canon Lock (Carryover)
- First Binding: pre-kingdom law event that encoded threshold closure roles.
- Original Compact: surviving First Binding law text and doctrine, not later commentary.
- Compact Orders: custodians, not original authors.
- Knight-Without-Title: inherited recognized role, not appointed office.
- Binding order remains fixed in law: Witness first. Warden second. Tyrant last.

Main Throughline
- The fourth flare is not a random emergence. It is a lawful challenge signal.
- A parallel tribunal jurisdiction exists beyond mapped northeast routes.
- That jurisdiction claims current crown and compact governance are procedurally invalid successors.
- Every expedition operation has two victory conditions: tactical success and admissible standing.
- Aric forms a provisional expedition tribunal under public witness terms.
- The Lord Marshall becomes conditionally playable under strict witness constraints because his continuity-memory access is needed to parse decree architecture.
- End state: the Fourth Tribunal is defeated in-field, but it records appeal into a deeper bench system the party cannot yet reach.

PART I: SIGNAL OF STANDING

SCENE 1: OPENING CINEMATIC - THE FLARE THAT FILED
Location: North retaining wall and outfield relay lines, dawn.
Featured Characters: Full party, Elowen, Iris, Selene, civilians.

Cinematic Direction
The fourth flare still hangs over the far horizon. It is thinner than it was at flarefall, cleaner at the edges, as if some unseen hand has cut it down from spectacle into procedure. Known nodes pulse. Storms spread. Fires consume. This thing waits.

Dawn over the capital does not look triumphant. It looks employed. On the north retaining wall, workers are already in motion with the flat urgency of people who survived the night and now have administrative consequences to complete before midday. Clerks carry ledgers between refuge coordinators and rail operators. Nadia's witness books from Chapter 4 are being copied into permanent civil archive by three relay scribes working in rotating shifts. No banners. No speeches. No one has mistaken survival for closure.

The crown ring beneath the city pulses once through the stone, and the sound reaches the wall after the light is already gone. Everyone pauses for the same half-second and then returns to work. That is what they won in Chapter 4: not peace, but a functioning rhythm.

Iris stands over a relay dish rerouted into the crown ring's acceptance geometry. She has slept perhaps one hour and is still more precise than anyone else present. The incoming resonance packet doesn't arrive like sound. It arrives like a legal pressure equalizing through a pipe.

Iris reads the glyph band, frowns, reads it again, and then says the sentence that shifts the room.

Iris: "It is not a threat emission. It is a filing."

Kain looks up from the north horizon.

Kain: "A filing from where?"
Selene: "From a jurisdiction we do not currently map."
Lyria: "Can jurisdiction do that? Reach beyond map and file itself into ours?"
Selene: "Apparently yes. Which means our map is not describing the full legal world."

Story Beat
The packet carries procedural language equivalent to summons and response schedule.
The capital is named respondent.
Aric is named acting witness authority under the charter established in Chapter 4.
The filing recognizes the city. It does not recognize the crown above it.

Aric takes the slate from Iris and reads the formal lines aloud so the record exists in more than one memory.

Aric: "Respondent authority required to appear in person or submit lawful delegate body within one interval. Grounds: contested succession, invalid inherited continuity, unauthorized emergency perpetuation."

Kain: "They have opinions."
Iris: "They have standing language. That is worse."

Elowen arrives before anyone sends for her, because Elowen has learned the posture of walls when the paperwork gets dangerous.

Elowen: "If this is a filing, then any response we make becomes part of the record it is building."
Aric: "Then we answer in person."
Elowen: "That answer needs named delegates, term duration, jurisdictional objective, and continuity structure here before departure. If you leave the city without successor process, everything you said in Chapter 4 becomes theater."
Aric: "Then draft them now."

He says it the way he said keep roads open in Chapter 4: no ornament, no uncertainty, just a decision made at the exact point where indecision would begin costing lives.

Selene turns back to the flare.

Selene: "This is not the enemy attacking us. This is the method asking whether we have the right to exist in its frame."
Lyria: "And if we answer badly?"
Selene: "Then it will not matter whether we are brave. Only whether we were admissible."

Outcome
- Expedition decision set: respond to flare as legal challenge, not only anomaly.
- City remains under witness-governance continuity while command cell transitions to flare response.
- Chapter 5's core premise established: existence is now under review, not just survival.


SCENE 2: INTERLUDE - DUAL SUMMONS, DUAL RISK
Location: Upper court logistics rail.
Featured Characters: Aric, Elowen, Nyra, Kain, Iris.

Cinematic Direction
The logistics rail has become the capital's second bloodstream. Runners come through every twenty seconds. Crates move north, medicine east, salvage south. Every person on the rail is carrying something that would have been routine a month ago and is now critical because the city no longer has spare capacity for routine.

Two sealed dispatches arrive inside one minute. Different satchel colors. Different authority marks. Same priority band.

Iris opens both and passes them to Nyra first. Not because Nyra outranks her. Because Nyra is the one in the group least likely to interpret language generously just because she wants motion.

Nyra reads both. The only visible change is that her thumb stills on the paper edge.

Story Beat
- Dispatch one orders Aric to remain in capital under emergency reconstruction authority.
- Dispatch two authorizes Aric to lead off-map response under witness charter crisis clause.
- Both are lawful on their own terms.
- Neither fully supersedes the other.

Nyra: "Both lawful. Neither complete."
Kain: "Required to leave. Required to stay. We do love continuity in this government."
Elowen: "No sarcasm on the record, please. At least not in the first copy."

Aric reads both, folds neither, and sets them flat on the rail rather than pocketing them.

Aric: "If I remain, we answer the filing with silence. If I leave without city continuity, the charter is false in practice. The answer is not choosing one duty over the other. The answer is finishing the structure first."

Elowen already has a ledger open.

Elowen: "Then name succession now. Local witness board. Term boundary. Reversal clause if you do not return on interval."
Iris: "And state objective in narrow language. If expedition objective is too broad, the filing court can argue we exceeded standing before arrival."

Nyra leans over the ledger and begins dictating constraints with the speed of someone who has resented bad emergency governance long enough to be ready when someone finally asks her to fix it.

Nyra: "No blank succession. No silent chain transfer. Named acting civil board: Elowen, Nadia, Thane, one rotating district witness. Any force deployment beyond city perimeter requires two signatures, not one. Emergency powers sunset automatically unless renewed in public hearing."

Kain glances sideways at Aric.

Kain: "You are making enemies very quickly with all this structure."
Aric: "Then they can object in writing."

Elowen allows herself the smallest possible expression of approval and writes faster.

Aric signs the off-map response. Then he signs the city continuity declaration. Then he signs a third document authorizing public reading of both before departure.

Aric: "If we hide process, we lose standing before we leave the wall."
Elowen: "Recorded."

Nyra reads the final structure one more time.

Nyra: "This is defensible. I dislike that we had to build it at this speed. I do not dislike the structure."
Aric: "I will take that as high praise."
Nyra: "It is. Do not grow attached to it."

Outcome
- Public declaration issued: off-map tribunal response with named delegates, term limits, and city continuity structure.
- Nyra's doctrine influences expedition legality from the start.
- Aric leaves under stronger legitimacy than he had at the opening of Chapter 4.


SCENE 3: MISSION 047 - FARTHORN CAUSEWAY
Location: Broken north causeway and flareward ravines.
Featured Characters: Aric, Zephyr, Rhea, Talis, Sera, support teams.

Objective
Secure expedition corridor to flare perimeter while preserving civilian fallback route.

Cinematic Direction
The north causeway was built before current flood tables and has been failing politely for years. The fourth flare ended polite failure. Half the western span is gone. The eastern support arches are intact in shape and useless in stress. Below the broken roadway, the ravine is filled not with water but with moving bands of dark script-light that bend around stone like current. The legal world has begun occupying geography.

This is the expedition's first lesson. The route to the Fourth Tribunal is not just dangerous. It is selective.

Combat Identity
Split-lane pressure mission. Forward team advances through adjudication zones while rear line must remain evac-safe, traversable, and recorded. The causeway becomes a long argument about whether movement itself can remain lawful under pressure.

Featured Threats
- Summons Husks: legal-proxy constructs that mark targets "non-standing," suppressing command auras and party buffs.
- Flare Jackals: fast flank predators that punish any healer or recorder separated from formation.
- Boss: Bailiff of Distance Karst, roaming adjudicator that relocates lane validity mid-fight and forces route revalidation under live threat.

Pre-Battle Direction
Rhea is already halfway under the first collapsed span before anyone gives her permission. She wedges a support brace into broken road tooth and shouts back without turning.

Rhea: "This will hold six people if they respect the order I say. It will hold no one if they improvise."
Talis: "Excellent. A bridge and a moral lesson."
Sera: "Rear lane first priority. If the forward line fails and the return lane is gone, this becomes ritual suicide."
Aric: "Then it stays open."

The party splits. Talis calls route changes from a mobile high point. Zephyr runs interception depth between forward pressure and rearline vulnerability, the only person in the field who looks relaxed while doing two jobs badly enough to kill anyone else.

Mid-Battle Notes
Karst's mechanic is not damage. It is jurisdiction. He stamps active stone lanes in red-black script and any ally standing on an invalid lane at phase turn suffers command severance. Talis counters by reading shift intervals and calling swaps in five-count windows.

Talis: "Left lane loses standing in three. Switch on my two, not my three. If you move on three, you are correct and dead."

Rhea anchors fallen bridge teeth into a temporary second crossing under husk pressure. Every hit she takes is another second of usable road.

Zephyr cuts down a jackal pack approaching the rearline ledgers and glances at Sera.

Zephyr: "This is the part where you say the lane remains clean and I feel better."
Sera: "Fallback lane remains clean. Keep it clean. If this turns, we turn alive."
Zephyr: "Excellent. Motivational enough."

Rhea slams a second brace into place with one gauntleted hand and does not bother disguising how much the impact hurt.

Rhea: "If any of you heroic idiots die on a bridge I built badly, I will take that personally."
Talis: "Comforting. Deeply comforting."

On the far side of the split lane, one support runner freezes when a summons husk marks him non-standing and the world seems to agree. Aric physically drags him back onto valid stone and pushes him toward the rearline.

Aric: "You do not disappear because a room says so. Move."

The runner moves.

Boss Phase
Karst enters center span and tears validity from the road ahead in rippling bands. Aric reaches him only because Rhea throws a brace into place under moving feet and Talis times the charge. The party cannot kill Karst on any lane. He must be brought down inside a lane still marked admissible, or the causeway records the victory as unlawful obstruction and collapses the route afterward.

Aric: "Hold him center. No finish outside valid stone."
Talis: "Valid stone is migrating. That is an irritating sentence."

The finish happens on the last admissible stretch before the phase resets. Karst falls, the route holds, and the causeway registers expedition crossing as lawful transit rather than breach.

Aftermath
When Karst drops, nobody celebrates because the bridge is still a bridge and bridges punish premature emotion. Talis keeps counting until the route stops changing. Rhea refuses to step off the support tooth she has been holding in place until three other people confirm load stability.

Talis: "Route fixed. That is not optimism. That is measurement."
Rhea: "Good. Then I can stop pretending these braces like me."

Zephyr finally exhales and looks back at the open fallback lane, where support teams are already moving like they had full faith this would work and not like they spent the last seven minutes one bad call from a ravine.

Zephyr: "I would like the record to reflect that I was calm the entire time."
Sera: "The record will reflect what happened, not what would improve your self-image."

Outcome
- Flare perimeter reached with expedition strength intact.
- Return corridor held and marked for noncombat traffic.
- Chapter 5's first mission teaches its central rule: winning incorrectly is losing later.


SCENE 4: INTERLUDE - THE SECOND CITY
Location: Farthorn escarpment overlook.
Featured Characters: Aric, Selene, Iris, Lyria, Kain.

Cinematic Direction
The escarpment drops away without warning. Below it sits a city that should not be there and yet carries the weathering, stain, and patient geometry of something that has stood through more than one age of naming.

Black-stone courts are layered in concentric rings. Towers rise by civic function rather than by defense position. Bridges hang between them, narrow and deliberate, their undersides carved with legal glyphwork older than current compact script. There are no outer battlements. No ornamental banners. No scars of siege. The entire place is shaped by one assumption: judgment is more durable than kings.

Lyria steps closer to the edge. The wind coming off the basin carries no dust. Just that same dry procedural cold from the flare.

Lyria: "It is beautiful in the way knives are beautiful. Entirely for purpose."
Selene: "Yes."

Story Beat
Selene identifies the structure as tribunal architecture, not military architecture.
Iris confirms the glyphwork predates crownline formalism.
Kain points out what everyone is already feeling: they have come to a court, not a battlefield.

Selene: "This was built to hear, judge, and survive jurisdiction collapse."
Iris: "No throne room sightlines. No parade avenues. No barracks priority. All function is record, transit, confinement, or declaration."
Lyria: "It was built to outlast whoever called themselves ruler."
Kain: "And now we are on the courthouse steps carrying a response nobody here wants to like."

Aric looks down at the ring structure. One layer in particular catches his eye: an outer stair wide enough for a public body to ascend but narrow enough to punish formation error.

Aric: "If this place is what Selene says it is, then the first trap will not be a gate. It will be a mistake we make because we assume it is a fortress."
Selene: "Correct. The enemy here is procedural impatience."

Outcome
- Expedition target confirmed: Fourth Tribunal complex.
- Strategic posture shifts from assault doctrine to admissible entry doctrine.
- The visual identity of Chapter 5's setting is established with full contrast against the capital.


SCENE 5: CHARACTER INTRO - CYR DANE (UNIT 022)
Location: Outer shield stair of Fourth Tribunal approach.
Featured Characters: Aric, Nyra, Aurel, Cyr Dane.

Cinematic Direction
The outer stair narrows as it rises, three lanes becoming two, two becoming one-and-a-half by the time the party reaches the first shield circle. Every sixth meter is marked by a faint ring in the stone where line discipline was once measured. The stair is not defended by archers. It is defended by geometry.

A captain in crownline plate steps down from the upper landing and does not draw to kill. He draws to correct. One shield rotation redirects Aurel's entry angle. A second cuts off Nyra's supporting line. A third collapses Aric's forward push into neutral footing without a single lethal follow-through. Two advance vectors vanish in eight seconds.

Nyra's hand goes to weapon, stops, recalculates.

Nyra: "That was adjudication line work. Old school."
Aurel: "And very good."

Aric raises open hand instead of drawing.

Aric: "Witness charter authority, capital expedition body, public response to served filing. We request lawful access and record opportunity."

The captain pauses. That pause is the scene's pivot. He removes helm.

Cyr: "Cyr Dane. Crownline Bastion. Former north adjudication line." He looks at the party, then back down the stair below them. "Your declaration was procedurally correct. Their response to it was not."

Nyra does not lower her guard.

Nyra: "You defecting or maneuvering?"
Cyr: "Defecting with evidence. Maneuvering to survive long enough to submit it. Those are compatible behaviors."

Story Beat
Cyr explains the outer stair is configured to bait respondents into unlawful first blood. Any lethal engagement on the lower circles invalidates standing before entry.
He has been waiting for a body disciplined enough not to trigger it.

Aric: "Why help us?"
Cyr: "Because they do not defend law. They defend continuation. There is a difference, and once you see it, staying becomes a kind of cowardice with polish."

Nyra studies him for one full breath.

Nyra: "And your evidence?"
Cyr: "Three filed objections. Two suppressed witness accounts. One sealed route order proving the stair was modified to force invalid entry."
Aurel: "That is either invaluable or expertly forged."
Cyr: "Yes."

Aric does not offer trust. He offers process.

Aric: "You get hearing. You do not get exemption from record."
Cyr: "Accepted."

Choice Node - Cyr's First Placement
A) Aric places Cyr immediately in the forward guide role.
B) Nyra insists Cyr remains under close observation until his first evidence submission clears.
C) Aurel volunteers to pair with Cyr, turning initial distrust into professional testing.

Outcome Direction
- A: Faster route progress, higher immediate trust risk, stronger Cyr-Aric line.
- B: Slower integration, sharper Nyra-Cyr friction, stronger procedural tone.
- C: Best combat cohesion early, stronger Aurel/Cyr mutual respect, slightly less political tension.

Aftermath Tone
As the party begins to move again, Cyr does not step into their center. He steps to the side lane where a defector who is not stupid would place himself.

Aurel notices.

Aurel: "You know exactly where to stand so no one has to ask whether you understand the situation."
Cyr: "Yes."
Aurel: "Good. That saves time."

Outcome
- Cyr joins as provisional guide and line specialist under recorded immunity hearing.
- Unit 022 playable onboarding established through witness-contract logic, not immediate friendship.
- Nyra gains a second doctrine-heavy ally who challenges her without resembling her.


PART II: THE FOURTH TRIBUNAL OPENS

SCENE 6: MISSION 048 - COURT OF CROSSING LANES
Location: Concentric court plazas and crossing writways.
Featured Characters: Aric, Cyr, Nyra, Cassian, Maris silhouette support.

Objective
Cross three court lanes and file admissible presence before hostile docket closure.

Cinematic Direction
The first court is arranged like a puzzle box built by someone who hated haste. Three writways intersect at impossible visual angles, each lane changing status according to rules etched in scripts too old to be decorative. At the center, a registrar platform rotates with slow certainty, counting the expedition down toward automatic default.

Combat Identity
Lane-control and condition-compliance operation.
- Lane A requires witness phrase completion under fire.
- Lane B fails if allied unit delivers lethal finishing blow.
- Lane C requires synchronized arrival inside narrow timing window.

Featured Threats
- Docket Wardens: condition enforcers that punish unrecorded actions.
- Seal Harriers: mobile disruptors that break synchronization.
- Boss: Registrar Fenn, who rewrites objective text during phase transitions and weaponizes confusion.

Pre-Battle Deployment
Cassian takes one look at the moving inscriptions and immediately starts verbalizing status to prevent silent failure.

Cassian: "No one assumes lane state. If I have not said it, it is not true yet."
Nyra: "Good. I dislike improvising inside hostile process."
Cyr: "Then you will enjoy nothing about this court."

Mid-Battle Notes
Lane A opens only when a witness phrase is spoken in full while enemies actively contest the speaker's standing. Aric takes the phrase while Nyra and Cyr hold formation on a lane that is trying to make honesty physically expensive.

Aric: "Present in declared body, under bounded authority, for record and response."
Docket Warden: "Standing unverified."
Aric: "Then verify it while I remain standing."

Lane B becomes the expedition's first hard no-kill pressure spike. Harriers intentionally drop low to bait fatal finishers. The correct play is restraint, not efficiency.

Cyr: "Do not kill what the lane wants dead. That is how the floor wins."

Lane C collapses and reforms in tempo windows too narrow to read from ground level. A hidden projection from the upper archive flashes correct paths across the stone at the last possible second.

Cassian: "Whoever is helping us, I respect their timing."
Nyra: "Respect them after we survive them."

One of the wardens attempts to provoke Aric into shortcutting Lane B by forcing a downed harrier into his finishing arc. Cyr intercepts with shield edge instead of blade and glances sideways without looking impressed.

Cyr: "The court keeps trying to teach us impatience."
Aric: "Then let it keep failing."

Boss Phase
Registrar Fenn rotates objective text live, turning "arrive together" into "arrive in lawful order" and nearly breaking the final synchronization push. Nyra catches the rule shift on the second line, not the first.

Nyra: "Witness ahead of Warden by one count. Move."

They do. The lane accepts. Fenn's platform locks open. Presence is filed.

Aftermath
The court does not cheerfully acknowledge them. It merely ceases trying to deny they are there. For this chapter, that is close enough to hospitality.

Cassian looks up at the upper archive shadow where the projection came from.

Cassian: "If our unseen ally is listening, their timing remains excellent."
Voice from above, dry and distant: "Your compliment is poorly aimed but factually acceptable."

No one sees the speaker clearly. That is right. Maris arrives first as competence, second as person.

Outcome
- Expedition presence filed successfully with admissible standing.
- Hidden upper-archive support identified as deliberate ally intervention, not accident.
- Chapter 5's middle structure begins: the tribunal can be fought, but only if read faster than it can rewrite expectation.

Escalation Hook
As the registrar platform seals, Iris catches a residual lock signature riding the lane reset and freezes it in projection before it can collapse.

Iris: "Next-sector access is writfire-gated in pre-Compact decree grammar. We cannot parse it fast enough in combat from outside the keyspace."
Cyr: "Whose keyspace?"
Iris: "Ashen Sovereign continuity hand. Singular."

Nyra arrives from the rear filing lane in time to hear the last line and does not pretend to like it.

Nyra: "Then we stop pretending preference is a plan. Annex 3 hearing, constrained deputization terms, full witness logging before first deployment."
Kain: "You volunteered that sentence with unsettling speed."
Nyra: "Because unlike optimism, procedure scales."

Selene checks the projection bands and confirms the custody seal trail.

Selene: "Annex 3 containment record is live and local. The tribunal already has him under restraint."
Aric: "Then we request supervised access in public language and bind terms before we ask for usefulness."


SCENE 7: CHARACTER INTRO - MARIS QUELL (UNIT 023)
Location: Suspended map archive over Court Delta.
Featured Characters: Maris Quell, Iris, Aric, Kain, Selene.

Cinematic Direction
The map archive is suspended between towers on channel-strands of pale blue pressure-light, part library, part instrument. No shelves. No fixed tables. Maps move through water-clear conduits across the room, rewriting themselves as route conditions change below. To walk through this room is to walk through a mind that believes location is a behavior, not a place.

The figure who has been helping them steps from behind a flowing projection plane, robes traced in route vectors, one sleeve marked with water stains older than the current day. She moves like someone whose balance is set by changing lines beneath her rather than by floor.

Maris: "You are reading this city like stone. Read it like current."
Iris: "You are treating legal resonance as fluid mechanics."
Maris: "Because under stress, that is exactly what it is. A jurisdiction under pressure behaves like floodwater with opinions."

Kain glances at Aric.

Kain: "We collect very specific people."
Aric: "Apparently because they are useful."

Story Beat
Maris reveals she has been sabotaging route invalidation from above because closure protocols would erase independent cartographic custody and collapse the only honest map of the tribunal interior.
She offers partnership in exchange for publication rights on all recovered tribunal records and route systems.

Maris: "If you win and then bury the map in another archive controlled by another authority front, this place will happen again somewhere else. I am not helping you win a prettier silence."
Iris: "Reasonable. Severe, but reasonable."
Aric: "Publication rights under witness charter. Source integrity preserved. Redaction only where living civilians are endangered by release."
Maris: "Add route provenance and authorship credit."
Aric: "Done."

Selene watches the exchange with open interest.

Selene: "You negotiated with almost no pause."
Maris: "If he had paused, I would have assumed he intended betrayal later."
Kain: "You are going to fit in alarmingly well."

Iris studies one of Maris's rerouted flow maps in silence long enough for the silence itself to become a social event.

Maris: "That expression is either admiration or territorial concern."
Iris: "It is analysis."
Maris: "That did not answer the question."
Iris: "No. It did not."

For one brief moment, they smile at almost the same time and then stop because both of them are too structurally composed to let the room keep the advantage.

Choice Node - Maris Covenant Framing
A) Aric grants publication rights immediately and publicly.
B) Aric requires Maris to co-author record release structure with Iris first.
C) Aric grants provisional rights now and permanent rights after the first tribunal victory.

Outcome Direction
- A: Fast trust, strong Maris loyalty, greater political fallout later.
- B: Best institutional coherence, strongest Iris-Maris partnership arc.
- C: Most cautious; preserves tension but risks insulting Maris.

Outcome
- Maris joins expedition as abyss cartographer.
- Unit 023 playable onboarding established through publication covenant, not rescue debt.
- Iris gains a peer who does not diminish her and does not resemble her.

Transition Beat - Route To Annex 3
Before anyone leaves the archive, Maris overlays lower-court transit flows and isolates the only admissible route to Tribunal Annex 3.

Maris: "You get one clean corridor to containment. Miss the timing window and the annex defaults to sealed review posture."
Iris: "Confirmed. Also confirmed: Writfire Chambers are keyed to the same Ashen Sovereign decree layer. If we skip Annex 3, we walk into the next mission blind."

Aric sends for Nyra, Caelia, and Thane on immediate relay. No one in the room mistakes this for trust. They treat it as custody engineering.

SCENE 7.5: TRANSIT INTERLUDE - ANNEX 3 CUSTODY CHAIN
Location: Lower witness corridors to Tribunal Annex 3.
Featured Characters: Aric, Nyra, Caelia, Thane, Maris, Selene, Vex, Lyria.

Cinematic Direction
The corridor to Annex 3 is built like an argument against improvisation: sealed corners, mirrored witness plates, and interval gates that open only after both route and authority resolve at once. Maris leads by moving exactly on pulse and never once looking back to see who can keep up.

No one talks for the first third of the walk. The silence is not discipline. It is dread with uniforms on. Every reflection in the witness plates splits Aric into older and current versions of himself, and for several steps he hates how quickly memory reaches for the old word before judgment drags it back.

Lyria, low enough for Aric alone: "If he tries to speak to who you were, stay with who you are."
Vex: "If he says your name like family, do not answer like family."

Story Beat
Nyra files respondent-necessity motion for constrained use of contained continuity asset.
Selene opens tribunal channel and binds hearing language to active witness record.
Caelia accepts primary kill-switch authority before first visual contact.
Thane verifies containment chain-of-custody seals and confirms no transfer breach since intake.
Vex takes rear guard without being asked, not to watch the corridor, but to watch the room they are all carrying into Annex 3.

By the time the annex doors open, everyone knows why they are here, who authorized this, and what ends the arrangement if it turns.

Outcome
- Annex 3 access gained through filed necessity motion, not back-channel leverage.
- Custody chain verified prior to contact with the Lord Marshall.
- Terms hearing posture established before Scene 8 begins.


SCENE 8: STANDALONE DIALOG - TERMS FOR THE ASHEN SOVEREIGN
Location: Containment dais, Tribunal Annex 3.
Featured Characters: Aric, Nyra, Caelia, Thane, The Lord Marshall, Selene, Vex, Lyria.

Cinematic Direction
Annex 3 is a room built to admit that some people are too important to kill immediately and too dangerous to leave intact. The containment dais hangs in the center on four decree chains. Suppression lines run through the air like taut threads of ember glass.
