## PART I: LAW THAT MOVES

### SCENE 1: OPENING CINEMATIC - THE ROAD THAT WAS NOT THERE

**Location:** Blackglass uplands northeast of Shattered Spire  
**Featured:** Full party (including Vex — subtraction happens Scene 7)

**Cinematic Direction:**

Dawn on clean sky. Three days east of Shattered Spire and the crimson fracture has finally dropped below the visible horizon. The sky is simply sky.

And **the road should not be there.**

Blackglass uplands are mapped as broken scrub. The party's charts show nothing. But between second watch and dawn, a road surfaced: black glass fitted smooth as frozen breath, running northeast. Three meters wide. No mortar in the joints. No settling cracks.

**It was not built. It arrived.**

The road reflects stars. Not the stars currently overhead. A different sky entirely, displaced by era or angle.

Aric crouches at the road's edge. Presses one gauntlet to the surface and pulls back immediately.

> **Aric:** "Warm."  
> **Iris:** "Topography delta exceeds natural thresholds by several orders. This type of Compact architecture has an emergence interval measured in centuries. It did not take centuries."  
> **Zephyr:** "So the earth did paperwork while we slept."  
> **Selene:** "Not earth. Clause."

Aric: "Define clause."  
> **Selene:** "Instruction text in the Original Compact that only executes when specific conditions are met. The seal at Shattered Spire satisfied one condition. The Knight-Without-Title's continued presence satisfied another."

Selene steps onto the road. Her feet find no give. The glass holds like bedrock.

> **Selene:** "The Compact does not build roads for armies. It builds roads for the things it is trying to reach."  
> **Kain:** "That is either reassuring or the worst possible news."  
> **Selene:** "Both."

A low resonant tone moves through the glass underfoot — not a sound exactly, more a pressure that travels through bones and settles in the chest. It arrives without warning and fades over six seconds.

Vex flinches three full beats before anyone else registers it.

> **Lyria:** "Vex."  
> **Vex:** (still, orienting) "It knows we are on it."  
> **Aric:** "The road."  
> **Vex:** "Not the road. Whatever wrote it."

No one argues. They form march column and move northeast.

**Location:** Mobile camp, glass ridge overlook  
**Featured:** Aric, Cassian, Selene, Thane, Iris, Lyria, Kain

**Story Beat:**

The party stops at a ridge break. Camp is minimal. The glass road hums at a frequency too low for consistent hearing.

The copies should be identical. **They are not.**

> **Thane:** "These renderings diverged. This passage, right column, third stanza — the obligation framing has changed to active present tense. It was conditional past in both source copies when I last verified."  
> **Selene:** "Conditional past means an obligation that may or may not apply. Active present means it is applying now."  
> **Thane:** "Law should not have timing conditions."  
> **Selene:** "This law was written to survive contact with entities that ignore static law. So the Original Compact was written to execute the moment conditions align, regardless of who is watching."  
> **Cassian:** "Then it was not only law. It was strategy."

Aric does not look up from the road horizon.

> **Aric:** "And we just triggered phase two."  
> **Thane:** "At minimum."

### SCENE 3: MISSION 031 - BASALT TEETH

**Location:** Broken fin ridge over Ember Chasm  
**Featured:** Full party

**Objective:** Reach three clause pylons before they sync.

**Objective:** Descend pylon shaft and disable resonance anchors before collapse cycle completes.

**Combat Identity:**

Vertical descent mission with timed pylon pulses. Every 90 seconds, the pylon emits a resonance pulse that applies vertical knockback. Characters caught mid-descent risk falling damage. Timing movement between pulses is critical.

**Featured Threats:**  
- **Ash Lurchers:** stealth constructs that emerge from pylon walls, applying vulnerability debuffs  
- **Clause Drifters:** floating decree fragments that lock skill cooldowns on contact  
- **Boss: Mawchain Executor** — binding enforcement construct with chain-drag mechanics that force repositioning

**Key Moment:**

At the second resonance anchor, the Mawchain Executor targets Lyria with a chain-drag that would pull her off the descent platform.

Vex intercepts.

He steps into the chain's path, catches it on his blade edge, and redirects the pull into the anchor housing instead of absorbing it. The chain shatters. The anchor destabilizes.

> **Lyria:** "That was not luck."  
> **Vex:** "No. That was geometry."

Aurel's **Sunforge barrier** converts the final pylon pulse into a brief radiant window, allowing the party to complete the descent without the knockback penalty.

**After Mission:**

The pylon shaft stabilizes. The resonance anchors are disabled. The path to Hollow Court reopens.

Selene examines the anchor casing.

> **Selene:** "This was not built to suppress the emergence. It was built to route it. Someone tried to control what they could not stop."

**Outcome:**  
- Pylon descent complete  
- Path to Hollow Court reopened  
- Vex demonstrates precision control without curse reliance  
- Foreshadowing: routing vs. stopping — a pattern that will repeat

### SCENE 4: RECRUITMENT - AUREL VOSS

**Location:** Sun Vault 7, collapsed intake gallery  
**Featured:** Aric, Cassian, Selene, Aurel, Vex

**Cinematic Direction:**

The Sunforge wardens have been running continuous threat response for months. The intake gallery is half-collapsed — stone that was stable last week has decided otherwise. Support beams lean at angles that should not hold weight but do anyway, the way things do when no one has time to let them fall properly.

Aurel Voss stands in the center of the gallery holding a conversion ring in one hand and a logistics slate in the other. His armor bears no blazon. Just use-scars and the specific kind of dust that comes from holding ground alone for too long.

He doesn't look up when the party arrives. He finishes writing something on the slate, sets it on a broken pillar, and only then turns.

He doesn't look surprised. He looks **tired.**

**Story Beat:**

> **Aurel:** "Which clause fired?"  
> **Cassian:** "Pilgrim sequence."  
> **Aurel:** "Then we are late by one cycle already."

Cassian goes still. Not visibly. The kind of still that happens inside first.

> **Cassian:** "How old are you."

It is not the question Aric was expecting.

> **Aurel:** "Twenty-four."  
> **Cassian:** "When did you sign."  
> **Aurel:** "Sixteen."

The silence that follows has weight.

> **Cassian:** "They should not have let you."  
> **Aurel:** (with the flatness of someone who has had this conversation before) "They asked if I understood permanence. I said yes. The compact does not require you to be correct. It requires you to answer."

Cassian does not respond. He is looking at Aurel the way someone looks at a version of themselves they are glad they did not become and guilty they almost did.

Selene, who has been reading the space between them, speaks before the silence becomes structural.

> **Selene:** "The intake gallery will collapse in eleven hours if the conversion ring fails. Aurel has been rotating barrier geometry every forty minutes for six days."  
> **Aric:** "Alone?"  
> **Aurel:** "The rest of my unit was redeployed to the third pillar two weeks ago. I was ordered to hold position until relief arrived or collapse made position irrelevant."

He looks at Aric without expectation. Just assessment.

> **Aurel:** "You are the relief or you are not. Either way I will know in the next sixty seconds."

Vex, standing at the perimeter, makes a sound that might be a laugh or might be recognition.

> **Vex:** "He is practical."  
> **Aurel:** "I am tired. Practical is what is left after everything else burns off."

Aric steps forward.

> **Aric:** "Will you join us?"  
> **Aurel:** "I was waiting for you to ask."

He picks up the slate. Pockets the conversion ring. Walks toward the party without looking back at the gallery he has been holding for six days.

When he reaches Cassian, he stops.

> **Aurel:** "You signed at eighteen."  
> **Cassian:** "How do you know that."  
> **Aurel:** "You carry it differently. The ones who signed older always do. You had time to know what you were choosing. I did not. That is not your fault, but I would prefer if you did not look at me like it was."

Cassian's jaw tightens. He does not apologize. He nods once.

> **Cassian:** "Understood."

Aurel turns to Aric.

> **Aurel:** "Where are we going."  
> **Aric:** "Cold Sun Vault. Then deeper."  
> **Aurel:** "Good. I would like to be useful to something that moves."

**Playable Unlock:** Aurel Voss joins as defensive conversion specialist.

**Character Established:**
- Signed at 16 (too young, knew it, did it anyway)
- Practical to the point of brittleness
- Does not want pity, wants purpose that moves forward
- Carries permanence differently than Cassian because he never had the luxury of understanding it first

### SCENE 6: MISSION 033 - HUSHBACK STAIR

**Location:** Spiral descent of mirror-salt stone  
**Featured:** Full party

**Objective:** Descend the stair that remembers in reverse.

**Cinematic Direction:**

Mirror-salt stone stores sound. Every footstep is held in crystal lattice and replayed in sequences that may not match the order they were made. First ten steps: ordinary echoes. By thirty meters, echoes trail three strides behind. At sixty meters, **you hear your own steps arriving before you take them.**

Vex slows at the stair's entrance two full seconds before setting foot on the first step.

> **Vex:** "Rear guard is wrong here. This stair remembers in reverse."  
> **Aric:** "You have an alternative?"  
> **Vex:** "No. I am telling you what it does, not asking to change the formation."  
> **Aric:** "Stay inside line-of-sight perimeter. Do not let more than one column gap open between us."  
> **Vex:** "Always."

**Combat Identity:**

Sound-lag mechanics. Enemy aggro keys off delayed audio. Move quickly = trailing echoes pull aggro to positions three seconds old. Stay still = resonance loops escalate to detonation. Correct rhythm is **constant slow movement**. Worst thing you can do is stop.

Featured: Stairway Resonants (echo-body constructs), Mirror-Hush Blades (assassins occupying the gap between sound and echo), The Reverberant (boss gaining strength from each echo delay beyond three seconds).

**After Mission Cliffhanger:**

Base platform. Standard roll call.

> **Draven:** "All present back to me."

The party counts down. Fourteen names. Fourteen voices.

Then the fifteenth.

> **Aric:** "Vex."

Nothing.

> **Aric:** (sharper) "Vex."

Nothing.

**Full stop.**

Kain is already moving back to the final ten steps. He crouches on the last step. Hand flat to mirror-salt.

> **Kain:** "No drag mark. No impact indicator. No blood. The formation log has no gap — he registered through the choke point and then his trace goes absent. Not blank. **Absent.** Like the sequence that would record him stopped recording."

Selene arrives at Kain's shoulder. She looks at the step. Her face goes to the particular stillness that means she is processing something she already partly knew.

> **Selene:** "This is a subtraction site. The stair contains an architecture class that removes sequence position. Not life. Not physical presence. **Position in the ordered chain of events.**"  
> **Kain:** "Meaning he is alive?"  
> **Selene:** "Meaning he may be alive somewhere that does not connect to our sequence. The site does not kill. It files."  
> **Mirel:** "Files."  
> **Selene:** "Archives the removed individual to a location outside normal temporal order. They continue to exist. They are simply no longer in our sequence of events."

Lyria: "Then we go back in and get him."

Aric's voice is steady. The steadiness of someone who has already run the options.

> **Aric:** "No. Not yet. We do not enter architecture that already removed one of us without a documented retrieval frame. If we run blind into whatever the subtraction mechanism is, we lose more people to the same process and have no one left to complete the retrieval."  
> **Lyria:** "He is not a frame problem to solve, Aric. He is Vex."  
> **Aric:** "Yes. He is. Which is exactly why the frame we build has to work."

The party stands at the base platform with the stair above them and **the silence where a voice should be.**

### SCENE 7: CAMPFIRE INTERLUDE - THE MISSING CHAIR

**Location:** Saltwind shelf  
**Featured:** Party without Vex

**Cinematic Direction:**

They make camp in the arrangement they have kept for weeks. Groundsheet here. Kit here. Fire position here. And then there is **a space where a person usually sits, and they leave it.** No one instructs this. No one remarks on it. The gap remains: a groundsheet less arranged, a ration kit unclaimed, **a silence where a particular kind of dry careful attention would have inserted itself.**

**Story Beat:**

Sera prays seated upright, eyes open. This is how she prays for an absence not confirmed. She is not asking for intervention. She is marking a person as present in the intent of her faith.

Draven sharpens his blade for twenty-two minutes. He does not usually sharpen this long.

Rook sits with his back to stone and his eyes forward and the set of his face is combat-ready except nothing is happening. This is how Rook carries specific grief: in a posture that could become action at any moment and therefore does not have to be named.

**Zephyr does not make a joke.** He sits near the fire and he does not fill the silence with anything, and for everyone who knows him, this is the loudest possible statement.

Mirel and Nadia inventory supplies in the methodical way of people who need to be doing something real.

> **Nadia:** "How many have you lost? Before all of this."  
> **Mirel:** "Still counting."  
> **Nadia:** "I asked about before."  
> **Mirel:** "I know. The counting did not stop before."

Lyria does not pretend not to be doing what she is doing. She sits in the gap space — not filling it but facing it — and says his name first. Because someone has to, and it should be someone who means it.

> **Lyria:** "Vex."

The fire adjusts. The wind continues.

> **Lyria:** "He does not get to disappear between sentences."

Kain does not look up from his blade.

> **Kain:** "Then we write the next sentence where he can read it."

Aric watches the fire. He has not said anything since the stair. He does not say anything now. This is its own statement, and everyone present understands it: **the decision at the base platform was the right one and it cost him something to make it, and both of those things are true at the same time.**

### SCENE 9: **NEW SCENE - THE WIT THAT FAILED**

**Location:** Fen waystation forge pit  
**Featured:** Rhea, Aric, Lyria, Kain, Sera

**Why This Scene:**

**This is Lyria's "wit fails" moment.** The most important character beat in Chapter 3 outside Vex's return.

**Story Beat:**

The forge pit is the only part of the waystation generating heat. Rhea dismantles and cleans her tools with automatic efficiency.

Aric watches her before speaking.

> **Aric:** "You came through the cult approach lane alone."  
> **Rhea:** "Their formation was readable from a significant distance. Once I had the lane geometry, the approach was geometry."  
> **Aric:** "Why."  
> **Rhea:** "Why what."  
> **Aric:** "Why here. Why this."

Rhea sets one tool down.

> **Rhea:** "I am not here for crown pardon. I am not here for noble wages or emergency field commissions or any other form of authority laundering. I am here because polished rooms call abandonment a tactical necessity and I have specific tools for correcting that language."  
> **Lyria:** "That is very specific motivation."  
> **Rhea:** "I have a very specific history."

She does not elaborate.

> **Aric:** "You will have command latitude while you are with us."  
> **Rhea:** "Good. I do not take orders from euphemisms."  
> **Kain:** "What do you take orders from?"  
> **Rhea:** "Correct assessment of the problem. Which your group has demonstrated once under pressure and multiple times in advance."

Lyria has been quiet. Normally she would have three observations by now, each one turning the room lighter. Normally she would have made Rhea smile against her will or at least forced Kain to acknowledge that someone else is better at dry delivery.

Normally.

She opens her mouth. The line is there. Something about polished rooms and unpolished people. Something that would land.

**It does not come out.**

Not because she is interrupted. Because halfway through forming the thought, she realizes she is trying to fill a silence **Vex would have occupied differently**. The joke is not for this room. It is for the room she wants to be in. The room where everyone is still present.

She closes her mouth.

Sera notices. Sera always notices.

Kain notices. Kain says nothing, which is how Kain says everything.

Rhea notices and, because Rhea is not someone who softens sharp edges just because they are sharp, says:

> **Rhea:** "You were going to say something."  
> **Lyria:** "I was."  
> **Rhea:** "Changed your mind?"  
> **Lyria:** (after a pause that costs her) "No. It just... landed somewhere else before I could say it."

Rhea looks at her for one full second. Then nods once, not sympathy, just acknowledgment.

> **Rhea:** "That happens."

Aric does not try to fill the silence either. The room holds it. Lyria sits with the weight of a joke that had nowhere to go, and for the first time since the stair, **she does not try to talk her way through the gap.**

### SCENE 10: FIVE WOMEN AND A BAD JOKE

**Location:** Wind-cut camp under split obsidian  
**Featured:** Lyria, Sera, Mirel, Nadia, Selene

**Cinematic Direction:**

The obsidian formation overhead is a natural split-arch: two angled slabs leaning against each other like two exhausted things that found the right angle. The wind cuts through the gap above without touching the ground below.

Five of them by accident. Lyria is repairing a cast glove. Sera has her boots off. Mirel runs supply count. Nadia watches stars with professional suspicion. Selene is still.

Sera has been visibly working toward something for ten minutes.

> **Lyria:** "Just say it."  
> **Sera:** "What do you call doctrine that cannot survive contact with reality?"  
> **Lyria:** "I don't know."

> **Nadia:** "Who taught her to do that?"

> **Sera:** "I have been practicing."

> **Mirel:** "We all remember."

> **Nadia:** "I keep expecting to be tired of all of this. The travel, the weight of what we know."

> **Nadia:** "Then we stop counting bodies as endings. We count them as evidence."  
> **Mirel:** "Evidence of what?"  
> **Nadia:** "That the thing we are fighting is not new. And therefore, neither are we."

## PART II: THE RETURN

### SCENE 11: **VEX RETURNS - THE ANCHOR RESTORED**

**Location:** Prism Tide Gate, Drowned Meridian resonance harbor  
**Featured:** Full party, Kael, Talis Quen, Caelia Vorn (celestial practitioner), Lyria

**This is the most important scene in the revision. Everything builds to this.**

**Pre-Mission Context:**

The party reaches Drowned Meridian resonance harbor — a district that went under during second emergence flooding. Pre-kingdom stone piers stand at irregular depths. Prism beacons pulse wrong: erratic competing frequency indicating multiple simultaneous command attempts.

The tide gate is a massive valve mechanism straining against pressure. If it blows, the resonance harbor detonates in uncontrolled pulse that wipes every active Compact signal for fifty kilometers.

**Including the signals tracking the subtraction site.**

Iris identifies this during mission brief.

> **Iris:** "If the tide gate fails before we extract the prism beacons, we lose the resonance trail back to the Hushback Stair. The subtraction site will be unmappable."

Lyria goes very still.

> **Lyria:** "How long do we have?"  
> **Iris:** "The gate is already leaking at two seals. Eleven minutes if we move now."

Aric looks at Lyria. She is not asking permission. She is calculating whether eleven minutes is enough to do two things instead of one.

**During Mission:**

The party splits. Forward team secures prism beacons. Lyria peels off toward a recessed platform where a figure has been holding three extraction corridors simultaneously with geometric precision that makes Iris stop mid-call and **watch**.

Talis Quen cycles positions on mathematically precise rotation — never holding ground long enough to become target, never abandoning corridor long enough for it to collapse.

Lyria reaches her during brief interval in tide pressure.

> **Talis:** "You chose the least survivable entry path."  
> **Iris:** (arriving behind Lyria) "We were operating without current geometry on this harbor."  
> **Talis:** "I know. Bold, if unintentional."

Lyria does not have time for proper introduction.

> **Lyria:** "I need a wayfinder. Now. Someone who can read prism resonance trails and backtrack a subtraction event."  
> **Talis:** (pauses, recalculates) "The Hushback incident. You lost someone."  
> **Lyria:** "Yes."  
> **Talis:** "Subtraction sites archive outside normal sequence. Retrieval requires three anchors: name anchor, witness anchor, and blade or sigil anchor. Do you have all three?"  
> **Lyria:** "Name: Vex. Witness: this entire party. Blade: he carries a curse-scarred weapon forged from failed witness-metal. If that is not an anchor I do not know what is."

Talis looks at her for one full second.

> **Talis:** "That is an anchor. But you need someone who can pull him through the resonance gap. Prism trails go both ways. I can read the route. I cannot walk it."

A voice from the upper archive ledge, dry and measured:

> **Caelia Vorn:** "Starfold doctrine can walk it. If the route is accurate and the caster is willing to scar their resonance permanently."

Lyria looks up. A figure in battle cloth with seal threads running the full hem length stands at the archive edge. Front-line variation. This is not someone who sealed buildings from behind a desk.

Caelia drops lightly to the platform.

> **Caelia:** "Lyria Voss. Your starlight signature was visible from the tide district. I was coming to find you anyway."  
> **Lyria:** "Why?"  
> **Caelia:** "Because I heard you lost someone to a subtraction site and you are the only celestial weaver in three kingdoms who could pull them back if someone gave you the path."  
> **Lyria:** "You said it would scar my resonance permanently."  
> **Caelia:** "It will. You will lose effective range. Possibly twenty percent. Possibly more. Starfold anchor techniques burn the channels they use. Once."

Lyria does not hesitate.

> **Lyria:** "Show me how."

**Post-Mission:**

Tide gate holds. Prism beacons secured. Talis maps the resonance trail back to the Hushback Stair with Iris confirming each coordinate.

The party gathers at a clear platform. Caelia draws the anchor geometry in the stone with luminous ink. Three circles. Three names.

> **Caelia:** "Name anchor in the first circle. I will speak it. Witness anchor in the second — the party must speak it together, everyone who remembers him present in sequence. Blade anchor in the third — his weapon must be represented. We do not have his blade, so we use this."

She holds up a fragment of curse-scarred metal recovered from the subtraction site threshold.

> **Caelia:** "Lyria will channel starlight through all three circles simultaneously and hold them until the sequence reconnects. If it works, the person archived returns to current sequence. If it fails, she burns her channels and he stays archived."

Aric steps forward.

> **Aric:** "If this fails—"  
> **Lyria:** "It will not fail because it cannot fail. I am not discussing alternatives."

She steps into the center circle.

Caelia raises her hands to begin the anchor sequence, then **stops.** Her eyes widen as she reads the geometry Lyria is instinctively forming.

> **Caelia:** (quiet, almost reverent) "You are forming a Tier 3 constellation."
> **Lyria:** "What?"
> **Caelia:** "The three anchors — you are not just channeling through them. You are **weaving** them. Celestial, Void, Shadow." (She looks at Kain, then at the curse-metal fragment, then at Lyria.) "This is Eclipse Compact. The Void Web constellation. No one has successfully cast this in two generations because no one has had access to all three sigils in aligned resonance."

Lyria's hands are already glowing. She did not plan this. But her starlight has always known what to reach for before she consciously understood why.

> **Lyria:** "Will it work?"
> **Caelia:** "If it does not kill you first, yes. This is why starfold techniques scar — you are not pulling him through a gap. You are pulling him through the **void itself** using the party's oath resonance as the anchor line."

Aric steps forward, alarmed.

> **Aric:** "Lyria—"
> **Lyria:** (not looking away from the circles) "I know. I am doing it anyway."

Caelia begins the cast. Her voice is steady, professional, utterly without ceremony.

> **Caelia:** "Name anchor: Vex."  
> **Party:** (together, every voice) "Witness anchor: Vex."  
> **Caelia:** "Blade anchor: witness-metal, curse-scarred, borne in the Hollow Gate and held at the Hushback Stair."

Lyria's starlight flares. Not the controlled weave she usually casts. This is **everything she has**, channeled through three anchors at once, pulling against a gap in sequence that should not allow pulling.

Her hands shake. Then her arms. Then her entire frame.

**The air between the circles fractures.**

And through that fracture, moving slowly as if walking through water that is also time, **Vex steps back into the world.**

He is holding his blade. The curse-light is still dark. He looks at the party standing in a circle around him, at Lyria whose starlight is burning channels that will not heal, at Aric who has not moved, at the space by the fire that they left for him.

He opens his mouth. Closes it. Opens it again.

> **Vex:** "You came back for me."  
> **Lyria:** (voice shaking, not from weakness but from cost) "You were never out of reach. Just filed poorly."

She collapses. Mirel catches her before she hits stone. Nadia is already working, checking channels, confirming damage.

> **Nadia:** "Resonance scar. Permanent. She will recover mobility but not full range."  
> **Aric:** "Can she still cast?"  
> **Nadia:** "Yes. Just not as far. She traded reach for retrieval."

Vex kneels beside her. He does not touch her, but he is **present** in the way he has learned presence is the only gift he reliably carries.

> **Vex:** "You did not have to do that."  
> **Lyria:** (eyes closed, smiling despite the pain) "Yes I did. You were in the wrong sequence and it was making my jokes land poorly."

**Vex, for the first time in the entire story, laughs.** 

Not loud. Not long. But real.

The party does not cheer. They do not celebrate. They simply **stand in the fact of his return** and let that fact be what it is: impossible, earned, and worth every scar it cost.

**Playable Unlock:** Talis Quen joins.  
**Permanent Character Change:** Lyria's effective casting range reduced by 20%. This is reflected in combat stats going forward.  
**Character Arc Milestone:** Vex understands belonging now. Lyria understands what she will pay to keep it.

### SCENE 12: **NEW INTERLUDE - WHAT IT COST**

**Location:** Harbor camp, night after return  
**Featured:** Lyria, Vex, Caelia

**Why This Scene:**

**Story Beat:**

Finally:

> **Lyria:** "At the fire?"

Lyria looks at him.

> **Lyria:** "Did you believe me?"  
> **Vex:** "No. I thought you would try and fail and the trying would cost you everything. I thought I knew how these things ended."  
> **Lyria:** "And now?"  
> **Vex:** "Now I know you are all dangerously competent and I should stop assuming failure is the only honest answer."

She laughs. Quiet. Tired. Real.

Caelia approaches from the shadow, not intruding, just checking.

> **Caelia:** "How is the range?"  
> **Lyria:** "Shorter. I can feel where it used to reach and does not anymore."  
> **Caelia:** "That does not fade. The channels remember what they were."  
> **Lyria:** "Good."

> **Caelia:** "Front-line doctrine would suit you."

She leaves them to the quiet. Vex does not move for a long time.

Finally:

> **Vex:** "Thank you."

Lyria does not answer with words. She leans her shoulder against his. He leans back.

That is the whole conversation.
