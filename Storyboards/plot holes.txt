Plot Holes and Narrative Gaps - Next Pass v1

Purpose
This pass converts open narrative gaps into canon-safe fixes that can be shipped across Chapters 1-4 without hard retcons.

Rules for this pass
1. Keep Prism canon intact: Prism is infrastructure. Oath-Prism and Covenant Prism stay player-facing labels only.
2. Preserve mystery while removing confusion: players should know the rules, not every answer.
3. Every major reveal must leave one forward hook.

--------------------------------------------------
Core Plot Hole Fixes
--------------------------------------------------

1) Lord Marshall, the Pact, and the Tyrant Vessel Problem
Gap
The Lord Marshall's corruption arc is foreshadowed, but timing and intent are unclear.

Canon patch
- The Lord Marshall did not "turn" all at once.
- He entered a staged pact after a failed relief campaign called the Harrow Winter Offensive.
- The Tyrant offered outcome certainty in exchange for legal entry through compact loopholes.
- His stated goal stayed "save the realm," but his methods became sequence-authoritarian.

Where to place it
- Ch1 optional lore: court audit fragments and private dispatches with casualty math.
- Ch2 mid mission: Elowen obtains sealed logistics records tied to Harrow Winter.
- Ch3 reveal reinforcement: Thane confirms pact language reused Binding-era clauses.

Implementation objects
- 3 journal pickups
- 1 war-map interactable
- 1 flashback beat (no full cinematic required)

Retcon risk
Low. Adds motive and timeline without rewriting existing scenes.


2) Compact Origins and the Binding Mechanics
Gap
Roles (Witness, Warden, Tyrant) are named, but ceremony mechanics are too abstract.

Canon patch
- Witness records vow and consequence.
- Warden enforces boundary conditions.
- Tyrant is not "jailed"; it is law-constrained and rerouted through compact terms.
- The original Binding failed because witness records were partially redacted by survivors.

Where to place it
- Ch3 Vault of Cold Sun: mural reconstruction minigame reveals role order.
- Ch4 opener: playable memory fragment of final Binding minute.

Implementation objects
- Interactive mural puzzle
- One codex page: "Binding Sequence Order"
- One short playable vision (2-3 minutes)

Retcon risk
Low to medium. Must align with existing Ch3 mural references.


3) Subtraction Points and Vex Return Logic
Gap
Subtraction points and Vex's return feel arbitrary.

Canon patch
- Subtraction points are compact checksum nodes where unresolved identities are temporarily de-indexed.
- A person can return only if three anchors survive: name anchor, witness anchor, and blade/sigil anchor.
- Vex returned because his blade held a stable witness scar from a failed prior Binding attempt.

Where to place it
- Ch3 dialogue chain (Selene + Thane) immediately after first subtraction event.
- Ch3 side encounter: "The Missing Footstep" confirms anchor rule in gameplay.

Implementation objects
- 2 dialogue injections
- 1 side event with choice gate
- 1 codex: "On Sequence Subtraction"

Retcon risk
Low. Explains existing event without changing outcome.


4) Tyrant Rebirth Architecture
Gap
Distributed rebirth architecture is cool but underexplained in operation and counterplay.

Canon patch
- Rebirth architecture has 3 layers:
	a) Memory lattice (history persistence)
	b) Oath relay (live host eligibility checks)
	c) Emergence gate (material return)
- It was built by post-Binding custodians who believed controlled recurrence was safer than annihilation.
- Win condition is not "destroy all"; it is "desynchronize one layer while sealing another."

Where to place it
- Ch3 late mission blueprint room.
- Ch4 strategy council scene with Iris and Aurel.

Implementation objects
- Blueprint wall interactable
- Tactical board cutscene
- Optional boss modifier tied to active layer

Retcon risk
Medium. Requires encounter design alignment.


5) Kain and Kael Shared Past
Gap
The thread exists but lacks payoff.

Canon patch
- Kael was Kain's former field-name used in compact black operations.
- The split happened during the Red Quarry Extraction, where Kain chose civilian rescue over mission objective.
- Current tension is identity debt, not simple betrayal.

Where to place it
- Ch2 campfire optional branch.
- Ch3 side mission: Red Quarry Echo.

Implementation objects
- 1 optional duel dialogue
- 1 memory object trail
- 1 reconciliation gate that affects combat banter only

Retcon risk
Low. Works as additive history.


6) Vex Blade Curse Origin
Gap
Blade significance risks feeling like a plot device.

Canon patch
- Blade was forged from failed witness-metal recovered after the Night of Broken Vows.
- Curse is a legal scar: it "remembers" unclosed compact clauses and reacts to void pressure.
- This explains why it can cut subtraction seams and interact with rebirth nodes.

Where to place it
- Ch3 relic chamber vision.
- Ch4 side quest: "Temper the Witness Edge."

Implementation objects
- 1 forge memory vision
- 1 upgrade quest with branch outcome
- 1 codex entry tied to completion

Retcon risk
Low. Clarifies current behavior.
