Chapter 3 Storyboard - REVISED PRODUCTION DRAFT
The Compact Unbound

Format Note
This is a full from-scratch Chapter 3 draft built as a production storyboard for cinematic scripting, mission briefs, and encounter design.
Core goals:
- Escalate from "we survived the seal" to "the system itself is alive and adapting."
- Add three new playable units with immediate gameplay and emotional utility.
- Center the chapter on chosen oath versus inherited script.
- End with a reveal that reframes the whole trilogy arc.

Canon Lock For Chapter 3
- First Binding: pre-kingdom law event that encoded threshold closure roles.
- Original Compact: surviving law text and seal doctrine from First Binding.
- Compact Orders: later institutions that inherited custody of law without authoring it.
- Knight-Without-Title: inherited counter-resonance role recognized by Compact Orders, not appointed by them.

Main Throughline
- Hidden clauses in the Original Compact begin activating around Aric, Vex, and dormant Oath-Bound sites.
- The party realizes the Compact is not only law text. It is executable architecture.
- Loyalist command fractures into emergency governance blocs; Aric is simultaneously mission-critical to one bloc and criminal liability to another.
- Mid-chapter, Vex is removed by a subtraction site that deletes sequence position instead of life.
- Final reveal: the Lord Marshall is a continuity vessel for the Tyrant line, an office that re-incorporates the same abyss intent across eras under different names.

New Playable Units
- Aurel Voss: Sunforge Sigil Oath-Bound. Defensive conversion specialist. Converts curse pressure into short radiant windows.
- Rhea Flint: Runebreaker sapper. Trap denial and breach control. Turns enemy geometry against itself.
- Talis Quen: Prism wayfinder and tactician. Predictive route control, extraction planning, and lane redirection.

Returning Focus Characters
Aric, Lyria, Kain, Sera, Zephyr, Draven, Mirel, Vex, Cassian, Selene, Nadia, Rook, Iris, Brother Thane, Kael (remote thread), Elowen.


PART I: LAW THAT MOVES

SCENE 1: OPENING CINEMATIC - THE ROAD THAT WAS NOT THERE
Location: Blackglass uplands northeast of Shattered Spire.
Featured Characters: Full party.

Cinematic Direction
Dawn on clean sky. Three days east of Shattered Spire and the fracture seam has finally dropped below the visible horizon behind them. No crimson pulse. No arterial glow threading the eastern dark. The sky is simply sky.

And the road should not be there.

Blackglass uplands are mapped as broken scrub and compressed ridge stone. The party's charts show nothing. The scouts' overnight reports showed nothing. But between second watch and dawn, a road surfaced: black glass fitted smooth as frozen breath, running northeast along a heading no current kingdom route follows. It is three meters wide. The joints between the glass slabs carry no mortar and no settling crack. It was not built. It arrived.

The road reflects stars. Not the stars currently overhead. A different sky entirely, displaced by era or angle, burns in its surface. Walking it would mean standing on a ceiling someone else is standing right side up beneath.

Aric crouches at the road's edge. He presses one gauntlet to the surface and pulls back immediately.

Aric: "Warm."
Iris: "Topography delta exceeds natural thresholds by several orders of magnitude. Emergence interval for this type of Compact architecture is measured in centuries. It did not take centuries."
Zephyr: "So the earth did paperwork while we slept."
Selene: "Not earth. Clause."
Aric: "Define clause."
Selene: "Instruction text in the Original Compact that only executes when specific conditions are met. The seal at Shattered Spire satisfied one condition. The Knight-Without-Title's continued presence satisfied another."

Selene moves to the road and steps out onto it. Her feet find no give. The glass holds like bedrock.

Selene: "The Compact does not build roads for armies. It builds roads for the things it is trying to reach."
Kain: "That is either reassuring or the worst possible news."
Selene: "Both."

A low resonant tone moves through the glass underfoot, not a sound exactly, more a pressure that travels through the bones of the foot and settles in the chest. It arrives without warning and fades over six seconds.

Vex flinches three full beats before anyone else registers it.

Lyria: "Vex."
Vex stands still a moment, orienting. He looks at the road surface, then up at the party.
Vex: "It knows we are on it."
Aric: "The road."
Vex: "Not the road. Whatever wrote it."

No one argues. They form march column and move northeast into a sky the road already knew would be clear.


SCENE 2: INTERLUDE - WHAT THE COMPACT DOES WHEN NO ONE IS WATCHING
Location: Mobile camp, glass ridge overlook.
Featured Characters: Aric, Cassian, Selene, Thane, Iris, Lyria, Kain.

Cinematic Direction
The party has stopped at a ridge break where the new road crests and gives a full northern view. Camp is minimal: groundsheets, cold rations, no permanent fire. The glass road hums at a frequency too low for consistent hearing. Everyone has learned to stop mentioning it.

Thane and Selene have spread two copied versions of First Binding text across a flat press-stone. Both copies were sourced from different regional archives, months apart. They are holding them side by side and neither is saying anything.

Story Beat
The copies should be identical. They are not.

Cassian crouches to compare specific passage blocks. He traces one passage with his finger, looks at the other version, and stands.

Brother Thane: "These renderings diverged. This passage, right column, third stanza — the obligation framing has changed to active present tense. It was conditional past in both source copies when I last verified."
Selene: "Conditional past means an obligation that may or may not apply depending on circumstance. Active present means it is applying now."
Brother Thane: "Law should not have timing conditions."
Selene: "This law was written to survive contact with entities that ignore static law. Static law cannot bind something that does not recognize temporal order. So the Original Compact was written to execute the moment conditions align, regardless of who is watching."
Cassian: "Then it was not only law. It was strategy."

Aric does not look up from the road horizon.

Aric: "And we just triggered phase two."
Brother Thane: "At minimum."

Before Thane can elaborate, Iris arrives from the relay array with a sealed spool in one hand. She does not hand it over immediately. She holds it and looks at Aric with the flat expression she uses when the information is structurally complicated.

Iris: "Priority legal dispatch. Dual-signature. Contradictory authority. Both seals are valid. I checked twice."

Aric takes the spool. He breaks the seal himself. He reads in silence for longer than the text requires.

The party waits. Lyria watches his jaw. Kain watches his hands. Cassian watches the road.

Lyria: "How bad?"

Aric: "Two Loyalist governance blocs have separated. The first has issued emergency compact authority request — they want me reinstated as acting field commander under crisis provision. The second has filed a crown writ: criminal negligence at Cinder Keep and unauthorized threshold engagement."

A pause.

Kain: "So you are simultaneously required and wanted for arrest by the same legal system."
Aric: "Apparently."
Kain: "Very efficient bureaucracy."
Cassian: "Can the blocs enforce either action independently?"
Iris: "Not with equal force. The emergency provision technically supersedes the writ under crisis compact law, but only if the crisis is formally declared. Neither bloc is the declaring authority."
Brother Thane: "So neither order is fully binding and both are fully real."
Iris: "Correct. It is a legal weather event. Both fronts are real, neither resolves until they collide."

Aric folds the dispatch and holds it for a moment before putting it away.

Aric: "They can decide what to call me after we stop what is coming."
Lyria: "And if what's coming doesn't wait for them to decide?"
Aric: "Then we don't wait either."

He looks at the group: at Thane's open texts, at Selene's careful face, at Cassian already running entry lines in his head.

Aric: "The clauses activating are not incidental to our presence here. They are activating around us specifically. Around the Knight-Without-Title role. Everyone in this circle should know that before we move another step."

Choice Node - Command Transparency
A) Aric informs full party that clauses are activating specifically around him.
B) Aric limits disclosure to command circle to avoid morale disruption.
C) Aric asks Iris to verify before disclosure.

Outcome Direction
- A: Higher trust across party. Sharper emotional scenes in Scenes 6, 8, and 17. Some morale weight to carry.
- B: Better short-term cohesion. Later trust fracture risk when the rest of the party learns from circumstances rather than from Aric.
- C: More data lines from Iris through scenes 3-6. Aric's delay creates a quiet distance Lyria registers and names at Scene 17.


SCENE 3: MISSION 031 - BASALT TEETH
Location: Broken fin ridge over Ember Chasm.
Featured Characters: Full party.

Cinematic Direction
The fin ridge is a geological wound: basalt columns split by ancient heat and cooled into irregular geometry, jutting from the chasm edge in blade rows fifty meters above a cavity that breathes. Ember Chasm earned its name. The thermals coming up from the gas fissures below are hot enough to distort vision and unpredictable enough to ruin a cast arc.

Three clause pylons stand at even intervals along the ridge spine. They are not constructions — they grew from the stone, faceted and upright, and they are vibrating at increasing frequency. The mission brief is simple: reach them before they sync. Synced clause pylons broadcast activation signal to whatever is listening.

Objective
Cross the fin ridge and secure three clause pylons before they sync.

Pre-Mission Deployment
Iris reads the pylon spacing and sketches approach lanes in the air with a light stylus.

Iris: "Sync window is approximately eleven minutes from current resonance rate. Three pylons. Three lanes. No margin for sequential approach."
Vex: "Split to three means no recovery depth if a lane collapses."
Kain: "Rear-counter on the exposed lanes. I take right, Vex takes left, Aric holds center."
Aric: "Lyria and Iris on pylon timing. The rest of you fill the gaps as they open."

Combat Identity
Terrain pressure mission with vertical knockback risk and delayed trap pulses.
Vex and Kain run rear-counter lanes while Lyria and Iris expose pylon timing.
The ridge columns provide cover but punish stationary positioning — Ash Lurchers target heat signatures that don't move.

Featured Threats
- Ash Lurchers: heat-bloom ambushers that rise from the chasm thermals and punish stationary casts. They track warmth, not sight.
- Clause Drifters: floating script nodes that intercept and rewrite active buff tags mid-combat. Units with active radiant or ward buffs become priority targets.
- Boss: Mawchain Executor, a basalt brute that uses anchor chain to drag targets into active pylon pulse zones. It does not fight by strength alone — it uses the terrain's lethality as its primary weapon.

Mission Spotlight Moments
- Lyria overcharges the second pylon's resonance to expose the Drifter node cluster hiding in its interference field.
- Vex intercepts a chain drag mid-arc and absorbs the pull without anchor damage, which earns a visible reaction from Cassian.
- Aurel's Sunforge barrier converts a pylon pulse into a brief radiant window that lets Aric reach the final pylon without taking a full hit.

After Battle
Ridge walk-down. The pylons are secured: silent, cooling, no longer vibrating.
Nadia works the casualty line while Mirel handles the compound-stress cases.

Nadia to Mirel, quiet, not looking up from a bandage wrap:
Nadia: "How many did you lose before this all got big enough for maps?"
Mirel is quiet for a beat.
Mirel: "Enough to stop trusting maps."
Nadia: "And now?"
Mirel: "Now I trust the people reading them."

Iris logs the pylon deactivation data.
Iris: "No sync signal was broadcast. We have a window before the next scheduled activation, duration unknown."
Aric: "Then we move." 


SCENE 4: RECRUITMENT - AUREL VOSS
Location: Sun Vault 7, collapsed intake gallery.
Featured Characters: Aric, Cassian, Selene, Aurel, Vex.

Cinematic Direction
Sun Vault 7 is not on the road's manifest. Selene marks it as a derivative site — a secondary Sunforge station that would have activated when the primary observation vaults detected a specific clause state. It is buried under fifteen meters of collapse and a sealed blast door that Cassian opens with a Sunforge geometric key, slowly, watching the internal pressure equalize.

The sounds reach them before the light does.

Impact. Counter-impact. A crisp, professional rhythm of someone holding a line. Not panicking. Not fleeing. Methodically denying every approach in rotation.

The intake gallery opens into a main chamber filled with script-animated wardens — Sunforge constructs that have been running continuous threat response for what the oxidation marks suggest is months. They move in disciplined formation. And Aurel Voss stands in the center of the chamber inside a tight conversion ring of his own making, redirecting every curse-pressure strike into brief radiant windows, using the wardens' own aggression as fuel.

He is not performing. He is working.

Story Beat
Aurel notices the door opening without breaking his rotation. He does not ask who. He asks timing.

Aurel: "Which clause fired?"
Selene: "Pilgrim sequence."
Aurel: "Then we are late by one cycle already."

Aric steps into the chamber. Two wardens break off toward the new presence.
Aric dismantles them cleanly.
The ring pressure drops.
Aurel ends his rotation and shakes out his hands.

He looks at Aric — at the posture, at the sword, at the absence of rank markings — and then he looks at Selene for confirmation. Selene nods once.

Aurel kneels. The motion is precise and without theater — a Compact formal report posture, not a gesture of submission.

Aurel: "Knight-Without-Title present. Seventh Relief reports active."
Aric: "Rise. We do not have relief conditions right now."
Aurel: "I know. That is why I said 'active' and not 'achieved.'"

Aric: "Rise."

Aurel stands.

Cassian: "Seventh Relief is a restoration protocol for sites that have lost primary custodian coverage. It activates when Compact architecture begins executing without supervision."
Aurel: "Correct. I have been here since the second pylon network lit. I could not leave and could not call out. The warden loop does not permit neutral exit."
Selene: "We broke the loop from outside."
Aurel: "Which is the method the Original Compact prescribes. It is also usually done with more personnel."
Vex: "We work with what we have."

Aurel looks at Vex directly. Then at the spiral mark on his arm.
He does not comment. He files it. That is his manner: observe, archive, act.

Aurel: "You are moving northeast. To the pre-Compact sites?"
Aric: "To what is still activating."
Aurel: "Then I am with you. Seventh Relief does not stand down while the clause chain is live."
Aric: "I cannot promise relief conditions."
Aurel: "Then we build them."

Playable Unlock
Aurel Voss joins.


SCENE 5: MISSION 032 - COLD SUN VAULT
Location: Subterranean star observatory.
Featured Characters: Full party plus Aurel.

Cinematic Direction
The descent takes forty minutes on a spiral ramp cut from pale sandstone that has never seen weather. The walls carry astronomical notation in a script Thane identifies as pre-kingdom calibration language — not decorative, not ceremonial, genuinely functional. Someone built this to track something across centuries and expected to still be reading it.

The observatory chamber is enormous: a dome of dark glass inlaid with a solar map on its inner surface, the stars represented not as points but as named offices, authorities, witnessing functions. In the center, a dais of seven mirrored pillars stands aligned to the dome's primary meridian. Four of them are dark. Two are flickering. One is burning at full radiance and triggering an automated self-scrub cycle that is slowly erasing the witness ledger fragments stored in the dome's passive archive field.

Time estimate on self-scrub completion: fourteen minutes.

Objective
Recover witness ledger fragments and prevent observatory self-scrub.

Gameplay Notes
Aurel and Cassian chain Sunforge barriers to survive the mirrored burst phases that trigger each time a ledger shard is extracted.
Lyria overcharges the two flickering pillars to interrupt the self-scrub cycle — but overcharging mirrors carries risk: it exposes erased witness names held in the pillar's passive memory, and what they show is not always stable viewing.

Mission Spotlight Moments
- Lyria's first overcharge reveals twelve erased names in the second pillar. Two of them appear twice, in different eras. She holds the cast long enough to let Thane record the resonance impression.
- Aurel reads the dome office-map during a lull and stops moving for three seconds before continuing. Nobody asks. He will say when he is ready.
- Vex intercepts a scrub-pulse aimed at the primary ledger repository. It costs him. He does not mention cost.

Lore Beat
The recovered ledger includes forty-eight distinct authority entries. But a recurring office title appears across separated eras with no gap in authority language, no succession notation, no retirement record:

"First Marshal of the Witness Crown"

Thane lays three ledger fragments end to end on the dais floor. The office title appears in each. The dates of issue span four centuries.

Brother Thane: "Same title. Same authority scope. No renewal. No gap. If holders changed, no record of transition exists in the witness ledger. And witness ledgers were specifically designed to capture transitions."
Kain: "Then either this office never had a transition to record, or the ledger was cleaned each time."
Brother Thane: "Either this office never collapsed, or collapse was staged."
Kain: "Either way, someone learned to wear continuity as a mask."
Selene: "Or they learned to be the mask. And the face wearing it became optional."

The chamber goes quieter than it should after that.


SCENE 6: CAMP INTERLUDE - FIVE WOMEN AND A BAD JOKE
Location: Wind-cut camp under split obsidian.
Featured Characters: Lyria, Sera, Mirel, Nadia, Selene.

Cinematic Direction
The obsidian formation overhead is a natural split-arch: two angled slabs leaning against each other like two exhausted things that found the right angle. The wind cuts through the gap above without touching the ground below. It is warm and dry and completely sheltered, and it feels like something the world is offering.

Five of them by accident — the men dispersed to perimeter checks and the particular isolation men choose after a long day of too much. Lyria is repairing a cast glove. Sera has her boots off. Mirel is running a count of the remaining medical supply tablets. Nadia is watching the stars with complete professional suspicion. Selene is still.

Tone Beat
Breathing room without tone collapse. This scene earns its lightness by having weight underneath.

Sera has been visibly working toward something for ten minutes. They can all tell.

Lyria: "Just say it."
Sera: "What do you call doctrine that cannot survive contact with reality?"
Lyria: "I don't know........"
Sera: "A funding request."

Shared laugh. Genuine, surprised, the kind that happens when a joke lands at exactly the moment it is needed.

Nadia, without turning from the stars: "Who taught her to do that?"
Mirel: "No one. She figured it out on her own, which is somehow more impressive."
Sera: "I have been practicing."
Lyria: "In secret?"
Sera: "I did not want to be bad at it in front of anyone who would remember."
Mirel: "We all remember."
Sera: "I know. That is why I waited until it worked."

A comfortable silence. Nadia sets down her kit.
Nadia: "I keep expecting to be tired of all of this. The travel, the weight of what we know."
Mirel: "And?"
Nadia: "I am not. Which I find more frightening than the Tyrant, honestly."
Lyria: "It means you found something worth continuing for."
Nadia: "Or I cannot tell the difference between purpose and momentum."
Mirel: "Maybe there is not a difference."

Selene has not moved. Now she turns.
Selene: "I keep seeing names doubled. Same intent, different bodies across different eras. I cannot tell if it is the Compact architecture generating the pattern or if I am."
Lyria: "What names?"
Selene: "I cannot read them clearly. But one sits near Vex's name in the architecture and it is not his."

The laughter is behind them now. They sit with that instead.

Nadia: "Then we stop counting bodies as endings. We count them as evidence."
Mirel: "Evidence of what?"
Nadia: "That the thing we are fighting is not new. And therefore, neither are we."


PART II: THE VANISHING STEP

SCENE 7: MISSION 033 - HUSHBACK STAIR
Location: Spiral descent of mirror-salt stone.
Featured Characters: Full party.

Cinematic Direction
Mirror-salt stone is a threshold material: crystallized from brine under sustained Compact-frequency resonance over geological time. It does not absorb sound. It stores it. Every footstep taken on this stair is held in the crystal lattice and replayed in a sequence that may not match the order it was made in.

The effect escalates. The first ten steps produce ordinary echoes. By the thirty-meter mark, echoes trail three strides behind. At sixty meters, you hear your own steps arriving before you take them.

Pre-Mission Tension
Vex slows at the stair's entrance. Two full seconds before setting foot on the first step.

Aric looks back.
Vex, low: "Rear guard is wrong here. This stair remembers in reverse."
Aric: "You have an alternative?"
Vex: "No. I am telling you what it does, not asking to change the formation."
Aric: "Stay inside line-of-sight perimeter. Do not let more than one column gap open between us."
Vex: "Always."

Combat Identity
Sound-lag mechanics. Enemy aggro keys off delayed audio — Lurches are drawn to sound-impressions stored in the crystal, not to active noise. A unit that moves quickly creates trailing echoes that pull aggro to positions three seconds old. A unit that stays still creates resonance loops that escalate to detonation. The correct rhythm is constant slow movement. The worst thing you can do is stop.

Featured Threats
- Stairway Resonants: echo-body constructs that materialize from dense sound-deposits in the crystal walls. They are made of old footsteps.
- Mirror-Hush Blades: assassin units that occupy the gap between a sound and its echo.
- Boss: The Reverberant, a sound-form boss that gains strength from each time a party member's echo delays beyond three seconds. Managing lag is the mechanical core.

After Mission Cliffhanger
Base platform. Standard roll call on completion.

Draven: "All present back to me."
The party counts down. Fourteen names. Fourteen voices.

Then the fifteenth.
Aric: "Vex."
Nothing.
Aric again, sharper: "Vex."
Nothing.

Full stop.

Kain is already moving back to the final ten steps, scanning each tread. He crouches on the last step. Hand flat to the mirror-salt.

Kain: "No drag mark. No impact indicator. No blood. The formation log has no gap — he registered through the choke point and then his trace goes absent. Not blank. Absent. Like the sequence that would record him stopped recording."

Selene arrives at Kain's shoulder. She looks at the step. Her face goes to the particular stillness that means she is processing something she already partly knew.

Selene: "This is a subtraction site. The stair contains an architecture class that removes sequence position. Not life. Not physical presence. Position in the ordered chain of events."
Kain: "Meaning he is alive?"
Selene: "Meaning he may be alive somewhere that does not connect to our sequence. The site does not kill. It files."
Mirel: "Files."
Selene: "Archives the removed individual to a location outside normal temporal order. They continue to exist. They are simply no longer in our sequence of events."

Lyria: "Then we go back in and get him."

Aric's voice is steady. The steadiness of someone who has already run the options.

Aric: "No. Not yet. We do not enter architecture that already removed one of us without a documented retrieval frame. If we run blind into whatever the subtraction mechanism is, we lose more people to the same process and have no one left to complete the retrieval."
Lyria: "He is not a frame problem to solve, Aric. He is Vex."
Aric: "Yes. He is. Which is exactly why the frame we build has to work."

The party stands at the base platform with the stair above them and the silence where a voice should be.


SCENE 8: CAMP INTERLUDE - THE MISSING CHAIR
Location: Saltwind shelf.
Featured Characters: Party without Vex.

Cinematic Direction
Saltwind shelf is an elevated platform of compressed mineral stone that catches the prevailing northerly and holds it. Everything on the shelf tilts slightly downwind. The fire leans. The maps curl at corners. The light distributes unevenly.

They make camp in the arrangement they have kept for weeks. Groundsheet here. Kit here. Fire position here. And then there is a space where a person usually sits, and they leave it. No one instructs this. No one remarks on it. The gap remains: a groundsheet less arranged, a ration kit unclaimed, a silence where a particular kind of dry careful attention would have inserted itself.

Story Beat
Sera prays seated upright, eyes open. This is how she prays for an absence that is not confirmed. She is not asking for intervention. She is marking a person as present in the intent of her faith because she will not accept that absences are final before they are demonstrated.

Draven sharpens his blade for twenty-two minutes. He does not usually sharpen this long.

Rook sits with his back to a stone and his eyes forward and the set of his face is combat-ready except nothing is happening. This is how Rook carries specific grief: in a posture that could become action at any moment and therefore does not have to be named.

Zephyr does not make a joke. He sits near the fire and he does not fill the silence with anything, and for everyone who knows him, this is the loudest possible statement.

Mirel and Nadia inventory supplies in the methodical way of people who need to be doing something real.

Nadia, not looking up: "How many have you lost? Before all of this."
Mirel: "Still counting."
Nadia: "I asked about before."
Mirel: "I know. The counting did not stop before."

Lyria does not pretend not to be doing what she is doing. She sits in the gap space — not filling it but facing it — and says his name first. Because someone has to, and it should be someone who means it.

Lyria: "Vex."

The fire adjusts. The wind continues.

Lyria: "He does not get to disappear between sentences."
Kain does not look up from his blade.
Kain: "Then we write the next sentence where he can read it."

Aric watches the fire. He has not said anything since the stair. He does not say anything now. This is its own statement, and everyone present understands it: the decision at the base platform was the right one and it cost him something to make it, and both of those things are true at the same time.


SCENE 9: MISSION 034 - LANTERN FEN
Location: Flooded ruin marsh, eastern approach road.
Featured Characters: Party without Vex.

Cinematic Direction
Lantern Fen gets its name from the Lantern Leeches — bioluminescent gas-bloom organisms that surface in standing water and produce a warm amber-gold light. From a distance it looks like a lantern in a window. Up close, in a ruin marsh with no dry ground, the light means the gas is surfacing and the water below is oxygen-depleted and the platforms are sinking, and the cult has been using the false-warmth to herd evacuation traffic into kill zones for weeks.

Iris reads the approach and adjusts route geometry twice before committing to the entry lane. Without Talis, she is running more calculations manually. It shows in the additional two minutes she takes before issuing the deployment call.

Objective
Secure evacuation route and stabilize the waystation while under cult pressure.

Combat Identity
Mobility-control mission with sinking platforms and gas bloom hazards. Platform stability degrades under sustained weight. Units must rotate standing positions or sink the ground underfoot. Combat zones shift constantly.

Recruitment Action Beat
The party is holding a three-lane bottleneck with one platform short and one platform flooding from a cult-triggered bloom. Kain calls the collapse before it happens. There is no solution inside the current formation.

Then the east runeline anchor — the enemy's lane control mechanism — detonates.
Then the second.
Then the third, in exact sequence, each cutting a lane the cult has been using as a reposition corridor.

Rhea Flint steps out of the smoke at the north breach point with two tools still smoking and a read on three positions at once.

Rhea: "If this choke is your plan, your plan needs supervision."
Kain: "You walked through cult territory to blow their own runeline."
Rhea: "Their runeline was the only thing holding their formation together. It was also in my way."

She does not wait for introduction. She reads the remaining platform geometry and starts calling positions.

Playable Unlock
Rhea Flint joins after mission.

Featured Threats
- Mire Stalkers: amphibious flankers using bloom-gas as aggro bait, drawing party units toward platform instability.
- Lantern Leeches: burst fear-gas detonation on ignition. Do not use fire-based attacks near standing water.
- Boss: Bog Tyrant Neth, flood-phase arena controller. Transitions the combat map to deep-water rules in phase 2, eliminating standard movement options.

SCENE 11: LORE INTERLUDE - FRAGMENT OF REINCORPORATION
Location: Flooded archive cellar adjacent to the waystation.
Featured Characters: Thane, Selene, Iris, Aric.

Cinematic Direction

The court fractures. Not physically — the stones hold — but along the architectural seams where the governance blocs have been applying pressure for months. Doors that were in the same building go to different hands. Messengers in the same corridor answer to different authorities. The Loyalist fracture that has been a legal abstraction becomes a physical fact in the upper district within six hours.

Elowen has been managing evacuation routes since the first pillar ignited, before any council convened, before any vote was called. She does not require a vote for this. She requires working roads.

Relay banners flare across upper towers in two colors, each carried by a bloc that believes it holds lawful authority. Street-level observers have no way to know which is current law. Neither do the couriers.

The banners are visible from every road out of the upper capital. People watch them and keep moving, because Elowen has been saying "move" for the last hour without explanation and something in the way she says it communicates that the explanation will not help.

Two couriers arrive at Elowen's corridor from opposite directions, overlapping:
"Emergency governance recognizes Aric of Cinder Keep as acting compact commander under crisis provision!"
"Judicial bloc reaffirms: Aric of Cinder Keep is wanted under crown writ for Cinder Keep collapse and unauthorized threshold engagement!"

Elowen does not turn toward either courier. She does not pause what she is doing, which is marking a route map for a civilian transport group and handing it to its lead.

Elowen: "Open every road. No permissions. Move people now."

She hands off the map and moves to the next group.
Behind her, the two couriers look at each other with the expressions of people who expected a clearer outcome.
Neither follows up.

Voiceover Cut — From the sanctum ascent:
Iris: "Distributed rebirth architecture confirmed across three capital sites. Vessel neutralization incomplete. Intent is decentralized and active."
Aric: "Then we stop chasing one enemy and start dismantling a method."

Final Image
The party emerges from the Bastion's upper approach onto the broken outer steps above the court district. Below them, the city moves. Above the court district, the three pillars burn. And beneath the city — visible through the fracture seams in the foundation stones that the emergence event opened in the moment of disincorporation — the old crown-ring glows, buried deep, perennial, patient. It burns like a second moon seen through the floor of the world.

Vex looks at it without speaking.
Lyria takes Aric's arm.
Kain says: "Northeast."
Aric: "Northeast."

They know what they have opened.
