# Oathsworn: Compact — Game Summary

---

## What Is Oathsworn: Compact?

Oathsworn: Compact is a story-driven tactical RPG with a deep gacha summoning system rooted in the game's own lore. Build a roster of powerful units, master turn-based combat that rewards timing and strategy, and fight your way through a world fracturing under the weight of ancient promises — one betrayal at a time.

Whether you're chaining brutal combos against a Rage Cyclops, curating the perfect unit roster for the Void Threshold, or uncovering the secrets of a kingdom built on controlled deception, Oathsworn: Compact gives you the tools to play exactly how you want.

---

## Tactical Combat That Rewards Skill

Battles aren't just about who hits hardest — they're about *when* you strike.

- **Queue your squad's actions**, then execute them in any order you choose. The sequence you pick matters.
- **Chain combos** across your units with frame-precise hit timing. Land attacks in quick succession to drive up a multiplier, then finish with a devastating blow.
- **Up to 6 units** take the field at once. Position them, stack their strengths, and adapt on the fly.
- Each unit has **physical skills, magic abilities, and passive traits** shaped by their role. Buff allies, debuff enemies, and heal wounds before the next wave arrives.

### Status Effects & Items
Enemies hit hard — and they hit dirty. Paralysis, sleep, stone, and worse can lock your units down mid-battle. But you're prepared:

- Curate a **personal Battle Bag** of up to 10 items before each fight — potions, restoratives, revive tonics — and pull them out at the critical moment.
- Skills and magic can cleanse ailments and turn the tide. A unit locked down this turn can still act the moment they're freed.
- Weapons carry their own danger too: every strike can roll a hidden status proc against the target.

Combat punishes passive play and rewards players who plan, adapt, and execute under pressure.

## A Roster Worth Building

Your units are your legacy. Pull for them, earn them, upgrade them — then bring exactly the six you need into battle.

### The Gacha System
- **Featured banners** spotlight new units with boosted odds and a fair pity system — no endless losing streaks.
- **Soft pity** kicks in after 60 pulls and ramps up your chances with every additional attempt.
- **Hard pity at pull 80** is your guaranteed floor. You will not walk away empty-handed.
- **Spark Points** accumulate with every single pull. Hit 160 and **claim the featured unit outright** — no RNG required.
- Switching banners? Unspent spark converts to **Archive Tokens**, a permanent currency you can spend on past units and rare materials.
- In summon UI, standard pulls are framed as attuning an **Oath-Prism**. Premium featured rituals may use a **Covenant Prism** presentation.

### Duplicate Units
Getting a unit you already own isn't wasted — duplicates convert into **Prism Shards** for that specific unit's upgrade path. Every pull contributes something meaningful to your long-term roster goals.

### Equip. Upgrade. Dominate.
Units grow with you. Layer on equipment for raw stat boosts, slot in **Materia** to grant new abilities, and level them up through combat. A low-rarity unit optimized by a dedicated player will outperform a neglected Legendary on a bad day.

## Rewards That Keep Coming

Playing Oathsworn: Compact means a steady stream of progression, special events, and surprises:

- **Crystal income** from daily logins, weekly challenges, story missions, and limited events — free players pull regularly, every 4-week cycle.
- **Dungeon missions** across a growing world, each with first-clear crystal bonuses and story rewards.
- **Archive Shop** with rotating selectors for past units and premium upgrade materials — spendable whenever you're ready.
- **Banner rotations** every 4 weeks bring new featured units, side stories, and challenge stages to test-drive the latest additions.

## The Lore Behind the Pull

In the world of Oathsworn: Compact, the gacha system isn't a mechanic bolted onto the story — it *is* the story.

**Prisms** are crystallized memory-lattices: the preserved oath-patterns of fallen defenders, bound into physical form so they can be called again when the realm faces collapse. They don't answer to anyone. They answer to **Oath-Weavers** — rare individuals whose sigil-bond is written into them rather than cast. A compatible Oath-Weaver touches a prism, and a defender answers.

In player-facing summon flows, the vessel is usually labeled an **Oath-Prism**. In high-tier or featured ceremonial summons, the same infrastructure may be presented as a **Covenant Prism**.

Every unit you summon was someone. Every pull is a compact.

## The Story: *Ashes of Oath* and *The Shattered March*

*Something is wrong with the kingdom. You just don't know it yet.*

---

### Chapter 1 — Ashes of Oath

You are sworn to serve a legend.

**Aric the Bold** — decorated general, shield of the realm — has spent his life earning the trust of the kingdom's greatest ruler: **The Lord Marshall**, a man hailed as wise, just, and incorruptible. Aric believes in the crown. He has bled for it. He has watched others die for it. He does not yet know that the Lord Marshall recognized what Aric was — an **Oath-Weaver**, rare enough to reshape history — long before Aric had words for it himself.

Then the reports start coming in. Raids near Whispering Woods. Villages burned in a path too clean to be random. Supply shipments stamped with marks that shouldn't exist. Sealed orders — signed with a familiar hand — that suggest Aric's soldiers were never meant to survive.

At his side: **Lyria**, a celestial sigil Oath-Weaver whose starlight magic bridges patterns that normally reject each other — the party's wildcard and its heart. **Kain**, a shadow assassin who trusted no one long before he had reasons not to. **Sera**, a holy sentinel bound by principle over orders. **Zephyr**, a storm caller who hides real courage behind bad jokes. **Draven**, a veteran warrior who lost his squad protecting civilians he never had orders to save. **Mirel**, a tide priestess who carries the grief of everyone around her before her own. And **Vex** — a cursed blade whose allegiance is anyone's guess, and whose curse may be the most important thing about him.

Together they'll cross burning passes, breach iron fortresses, and unravel a conspiracy that reaches deeper than any of them imagined.

Seven allies. Twelve missions. One man who may have been using them all along.

*The truth is waiting inside Cinder Keep. Aric won't like what he finds.*

---

### Chapter 2 — The Shattered March

*The sky is bleeding. The kingdom is fracturing. And the thing below the Hollow Gate has been waiting longer than any kingdom has existed.*

The Lord Marshall's ritual tears a crimson fracture across the sky above Cinder Keep. Aric's party — stripped of rank, branded expendable — marches east toward Shattered Spire, the only structure old enough to have answers.

What they find there is larger than betrayal. An ancient compact predating the kingdom. A Void Tyrant suppressed once before and awakening again. A designation written into Aric before he was old enough to know what an oath was: **Knight-Without-Title** — the role the compact was built around, the key the threshold was built to receive.

New allies join the march: **Cassian**, an Oath-Bound Warden who gave up choice itself at the binding; **Selene**, a vale seer who speaks in thread and consequence; **Kael**, a deep-cover operative who chose a side when no contract told him to; **Nadia**, a tide-blooded cleric who evacuated sixty-eight civilians alone before the party arrived; **Rook**, a warrior who held the Gate's interior for three days and does not believe this requires explanation; and **Iris**, the sole survivor of Research Expedition Seven, armed with eleven months of data on the Tyrant and a personal interest in its ending.

And **Vex** — who walked out of the void below Cinder Keep and back into the firelight, curse-light gone dark, still standing — becomes something none of them predicted: the key the compact's lock was built to recognize.

*The fracture can be sealed. The compact survives the sealing. And someone, somewhere, already has their hands on what comes next.*
