## Why Play Oathsworn: Compact?

### You have options. Here is why this one is different.

---

### 1. The gacha system *is* the story.

Most games with gacha systems treat summoning as a separate layer — a shop dressed in anime art and rarity colors. Oathsworn does something different.

In this world, **Prisms** are crystallized memory-lattices: the preserved oath-patterns of fallen defenders, sealed into physical form so they can be called again when the realm faces extinction. They don't answer to money. They don't answer to commands. They answer to **Oath-Weavers** — rare individuals whose sigil-bond is written into them rather than cast by them. A compatible Oath-Weaver touches a prism, and a defender answers.

Every unit you summon was someone. Every pull is a compact.

That means the character you just added to your roster has a *reason* to be there. A lore reason. The game earns every name in your collection.

---

### 2. A gacha economy built to respect your time.

- **Soft pity at 60 pulls** — your odds climb with every attempt after that.
- **Hard pity at 80** — guaranteed floor. Always. No horror stories.
- **Spark Points** accumulate every single pull. Hit **160** and pick the featured unit outright. No RNG required.
- **Archive Tokens** convert unspent spark so nothing you earned disappears when a banner ends.
- **Duplicate units** become Prism Shards — exchangeable for Epic and Legendary selectors. Every pull gives you something that matters.

Free players pull consistently every cycle. Spending accelerates — it doesn't gatekeep.

---

### 3. Oath-Weaving is the signature system.

This is the system that sets Oathsworn: Compact apart.

Every unit carries a sigil identity. At mission start, compatible sigil pairings trigger **passive resonance** — small, meaningful stat boosts that reward composition planning. Then, when an Oath-Weaver steps in, the fight changes.

- **Dedicated Weave panel in battle** with live previews of target type, power profile, and status payload.
- **Single-target weave flow built for speed**: choose weave, tap enemy, auto-queue, panel closes.
- **Mission-limited weave charges** force high-impact decision-making instead of spam.
- **Every compatible sigil pair is a distinct recipe** with unique behavior loaded from data.
- **Resonance Codex progression** unlocks in-story and tracks discovered weaves, tying mastery to narrative progression.

You are not just selecting skills. You are composing oath patterns under pressure.

That feeling — of building a party that *fits* and then activating the exact weave at the exact moment — is the core fantasy of this game.

---

### 4. Combat rewards planning, execution, and nerve.

Other titles let you auto-battle your way to endgame. Oathsworn asks more — and gives more back.

- **Queue your squad's six actions, then execute in any order you choose.** Sequence is strategy.
- **Frame-precise chaining.** Land hits in quick succession across your units to build a combo multiplier, then cash out with finishers or control effects.
- **Status pressure is real** — paralysis, sleep, stone, and break effects punish sloppy turns.
- **Battle Bag loadout system** (up to 10 pre-selected items) turns item choice into pre-mission strategy, not panic UI.
- **Tap-to-target battlefield control** with direct enemy selection and clear above-head target indicators.

A low-rarity unit in the hands of a tactical player will outperform a Legendary left on default.

---

### 5. A cast you will actually care about.

Oathsworn's characters are not archetypes wearing different outfits.

**Aric** is a man who believed in something and was wrong, and the story does not give him the mercy of easy vindication. His arc from decorated general to Knight-Without-Title is earned across every mission.

**Lyria** is the emotional core of the party — but she has her own identity as a celestial sigil Oath-Weaver whose magic bridges patterns that reject each other. Her wit is armor. The game shows you what it's protecting.

**Vex** arrives broken and silent and carries the chapter's deepest arc without making a speech about it. What he becomes is shown, not told.

**Rook** held the inside of the Hollow Gate alone for three days. Nobody asked him to. He didn't think it required explaining.

Every character in the roster — playable and summonable — was someone with a reason. The game respects that, and so will you.

---

### 6. A story that trusts you.

Oathsworn does not over-explain. It does not walk you through its twists or pause to make sure you understood. It plants the Lord Marshall's hand on Aric's shoulder in frame one, and then it waits for you to feel the weight of that same hand pressing down.

The story is built across two full chapters with a third in development. Each chapter is a contained arc with its own theme and conclusion. No seasonal filler. No spinning-wheel narrative. A real story, told to completion, chapter by chapter.

*The compact does not retire.* Neither does the commitment to the story.

---

### 7. It keeps growing.

Oathsworn: Compact is the opening of a series, not a closed story. The mythology established in Chapters 1 and 2 — the Oath-Weave system, the prism infrastructure, the compact's counter-resonance designation, the third emergence Selene cannot yet read clearly — is infrastructure for a world that expands with every chapter.

The roster expands through summoning and story alike. Characters you meet in cutscenes become characters you can field. The people you earned are the people who matter.
