## I. The Compact, In Plain Terms

[Hard Canon]
The Compact is an ancient oath-law framework that predates the current kingdom and governs threshold containment, sigil roles, and recurrence control related to the Abyss Tyrant cycle.

[Hard Canon]
The Compact is not only a treaty or institution. It is a legal-resonant system where record, duty, and boundary function as enforceable metaphysical law.

[Hard Canon]
The Compact survives closures. Sealing an emergence does not retire its obligations.

---

## II. The Compact Law (Current Recoverable Form)

This section is the closest current reconstruction of compact law from story evidence, presented as it has been recovered through dialogue and discovery across the storyboards.

[Hard Canon]
The binding order is the foundation. Witness first. Warden second. Tyrant last. This is not custom or preference—it is the structure itself. 

[Hard Canon]
No witness record means no lawful vow state to begin with. No warden state means no enforceable boundary. The Tyrant constraint only becomes lawful once the first two states exist. And that is not weakness—that is law.

[Hard Canon]
Record continuity failure, not simple lack of force, is what has broken the compact before. When the witness record is compromised, compact law stops being legal constraint and becomes raw power contest.

[Hard Canon]
For those sworn under the Compact—Oath-Bound personnel—the binding ceremony imposes specific choice constraints under critical containment conditions:
- Cannot retreat once a fracture is assessed critical.
- Cannot redirect service to sovereign authority during active threshold containment.
- Cannot refuse return when sent under compact obligation.

[Hard Canon]
These constraints are lifetime. Whether a binding is freely entered or coerced through institutional mandate depends on the historical period and sovereign authority at time of swearing. Current era bindings are presented as voluntary entry with informed cost acceptance, but historical precedent shows institutional coercion as a recurring pattern.

[Hard Canon]
The Compact can issue claims as legal force events. A prosecution judgment strike is not ordinary injury. It does not heal like living restoration magic heals wounds.

[Hard Canon]
Closure does not void the role. The Knight-Without-Title persists in the law long after the emergence is sealed. The Compact remembers what flesh forgets.

## III. Terms of Binding (Operational Summary)

[Hard Canon]
Binding is role-law assignment, not just ritual power transfer.

[Hard Canon]
A stable binding requires:
- A valid witness record.
- A designated warden line capable of maintaining boundary law.
- A constrained tyrant state inside named terms.
- A Knight-Without-Title seal carrying the witness authority in codified presence and signature.

[Hard Canon]
The Knight-Without-Title functions as the binding's living legal witness—not merely a combatant role, but the legal seal that validates and maintains the entire compact structure. Without this seal present and active, the binding's legal force degrades toward raw power contest.

[Hard Canon]
Modern compact orders inherit custody of older designations rather than authoring them from scratch. Current bearers maintain roles codified in original binding architecture.

## IV. Knight-Without-Title

### What It Is

[Hard Canon]
Knight-Without-Title is an original compact designation tied to threshold closure and binding witness authority.

[Hard Canon]
The designation refers to an agent whose function is not merely to carry a counter-resonance key, but to *be* the key—the living legal witness whose presence and signature codify the binding's authority. Role and person collapse into one designation state.

[Hard Canon]
The designation attached to Aric predates current institutions and was written at the first binding before current courts/orders inherited custody.

[Hard Canon]
Knight-Without-Title is singular per recurrence age. Only one legal index is active at the time of binding crisis.

[Hard Canon]
The role persists in legal-resonant form through closures and new emergences, waiting for the next age when witness authority is needed.

### Practical Meaning

[Hard Canon]
The Knight-Without-Title is the binding's legal seal—a living charter whose presence validates witness-record authority. Without it, compact law weakens into institutional power struggle.

[Hard Canon]
Aric's role as original Knight-Without-Title means he was the first witness, and that witnessing locked him into the binding permanently. His signature is written into the compact's legal structure.

[Working Canon]
This is why the Tyrant identifies Aric by designation instead of name.

## V. Original Binding Participants

### The Locked Roster

[Hard Canon]
The first binding named three participant roles:
- **Witness**: Aric
- **Warden**: The First Celestial Oath-Weaver (original guardian of the Compact Order, celestial-sigil authority)
- **Tyrant**: Original Abyss manifestation (current Tyrant is a new vessel through institutional inheritance, not ancestral descent)

### The Warden's Loss

[Hard Canon]
The original Warden's record line was celestial-marked and served as transcendent legal authority. When the Defiler corrupted the Tyrant's record section, institutional law weakened but could not fully break the celestial witness line:

> "When the Tyrant changed the records, not even her heavens could hold back the Abyss."

The Defiler's redactions severed the connection between witness authority and containment function, but the Warden's celestial linkage persisted as a broken circuit—power without operational law.

### Current Inheritance

[Hard Canon]
Lyria, as a rare Celestial Oath-Weaver, carries the lineage capability of the original Warden role. Discovery of this inheritance forms a foundational arc for her character—she must grow into the legal responsibility of maintaining warden-line authority through her celestial channel.

## VI. Sigil Lore (and Blade Anchors)

### Nature of Sigils

[Hard Canon]
Oath sigils are vow-linked resonance marks, politically controlled and strategically significant.

[Hard Canon]
Most people cannot safely carry true oath sigils; failed forcing can cause oath-burn/channel collapse.

[Hard Canon]
Not all frontline combatants are full Oath-Weavers; many operate via training, relic support, or partial resonance methods.

### Blade Anchors and Resonance Keys

[Hard Canon]
Certain weapons function as resonance anchors bound to sigil authority and compact law. Vex's blade is one such anchor, but multiple blade-class anchors exist across different sigil traditions and institutional custody.

[Hard Canon]
A blade anchor does not operate like ordinary equipment. It carries stored oath-resonance and responds to compatible weaver presence.

### Sigil Classes in Current System

[Hard Canon]
Current game system defines ten sigil families:
- Celestial
- Void
- Light
- Tide
- Shadow
- Storm
- Ember
- Rift
- Earth
- Volt

[Working Canon]
Sigil compatibility enables passive resonance and active weave expressions, with Celestial lines currently acting as high-bridge channels in mixed teams.

## VII. Oath-Weaving Lore

### Definition

[Hard Canon]
Oath-Weaving is ancient vow-tech formalized after the Night of Broken Vows, joining oath rites and star-chart law to stabilize war aether.

[Hard Canon]
Oath-Weaver status is not ordinary spellcasting. It is a promise-state written into the bearer rather than cast on demand.

### Current Character Grounding

[Hard Canon]
Lyria is a rare Celestial Oath-Weaver with unusually broad cross-sigil compatibility and the lineage capacity to inherit and develop into the Warden role.

[Hard Canon]
Aric is the original Knight-Without-Title—Oath-Weaver-capable with binding witness authority codified in his presence and signature.

[Hard Canon]
Caelia is a full Oath-Weaver with growing channel capacity and weave-expression capability.

[Hard Canon]
Code-state gameplay currently flags Aric, Lyria, and Caelia as weave-enabled identities.

### Functional Constraints

[Hard Canon]
Weaving in narrative and system context is both tactical action and legal interaction with compact structure, not purely elemental output. Oath-Weavers are living channels for binding authority, not spell-casters.

## VIII. Prism and Summoning Lore

[Hard Canon]
Prisms are crystallized memory-lattices storing oath-pattern imprints and resonance templates for defender recall.

[Hard Canon]
A prism does not answer ordinary command. Stable manifestation requires Oath-Weaver presence plus compatible sigil resonance.

## IX. Recurrence Model (Abyss Tyrant)

[Hard Canon]
Recurrence is cyclical and operates through vessel inheritance—each era manifests a new Abyss Tyrant through institutional anchors and binding structure re-entry, not through single-body immortality.

[Hard Canon]
The original Tyrant does not persist as a single consciousness across eras. Instead, the role itself carries forward through surviving anchors (records, oaths, and institutional custody) and emerges anew with each binding crisis.

[Hard Canon]
The Lord Marshall reveal path frames this model: identities/titles change by era while the tyrant-binding structure persists. Each manifestation is a new vessel shaped by the era's institutional context, not a reincarnation of a single original entity.

[Hard Canon]
Without the Compact's binding structure and witness authority, the cycle would not perpetuate. The law creates the recurrence—destruction of the Compact is the only means to void it permanently.
